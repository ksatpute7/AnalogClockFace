Identify Narrative Features in Your Own Narrative

Now, you are going to look for narrative features in the Narrative Cold Writes that you wrote earlier this year.

Reread your Narrative Cold Write. As you read your piece, be on the lookout for the features we have learned about: setting, narrator, characters, events, and problem/solution.

Now, begin completing your personal graphic organizer. Refer to the chart the class completed in the last task to help you with your work. When you have done as much as you can, swap Cold Writes with your partner. Give each other feedback and help to complete your graphic organizer.

Use these questions to guide you.

  • What features do you notice in your piece?
  • Is there a clear setting (time and place) in your piece? If not, decide on one now and add it in.
  • What about characters? Are all the characters important to the story? Did you include all the important characters?
  • What are the main events? What is the order of events?

You and your partner can revise these features together.
