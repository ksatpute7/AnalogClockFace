Day 3: Spelling Search

  • Point out that the words students are learning this week have the VCCV pattern.

  • Discuss how recognizing the syllables in a word can help them become stronger readers, writers, and spellers. Review dividing the word between the consonants when two consonants are in the middle of a word. Ask the following questions.

    • What do we call a syllable that ends with a consonant? (closed)
    • What vowel sound should you expect in a closed syllable? (short)

  • Tell students to do a Spelling Search.

  • Ask them to use a text they are currently reading, one they have already read, or one provided by you. The goal is to find words in the text that fit the spelling patterns and add these to the Spelling Notebook.

  • Invite a few students to share the words they found and then syllabicate them with the whole class.
