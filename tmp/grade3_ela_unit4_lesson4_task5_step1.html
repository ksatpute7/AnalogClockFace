Punctuating Dialogue

Authors use punctuation to let readers know that they are reading dialogue. Quotation marks let the reader know that a character is speaking.

You also have to pay attention to other punctuation marks (commas, periods, question marks, and exclamation points) when you punctuate dialogue.

  • Look through Gram’s Angel Food Cake with your partner for examples of different ways to punctuate dialogue. Share what you find with the class.
  • Now, practice punctuating dialogue. Look at the examples of dialogue with the punctuation marks missing. Write these sentences in your Notebook, and punctuate them properly.

Remember, when you write dialogue in your own narratives, it is important to give your reader these punctuation clues so that they know when a character is speaking.
