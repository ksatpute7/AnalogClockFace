Conjunctions

You will now learn about a group of words called conjunctions. Conjunctions have a very important job; they allow a writer to combine ideas to make his or her intended meaning clear to the reader.

  • Try using a few conjunctions. How would you put these sentences together?
    • I like apples. I like bananas better.
  • What conjunction would go in the following sentence?
    • I would like either an apple ___ a banana.
  • Try putting these sentences together without using and, or, or but.
    • I finished my homework.
    • I got to watch television.
  • Read the following sentence:
    • Many communities are interconnected, or joined to one another, because people know what other people in faraway places are doing.
    • Identify the conjunctions and explain what each does.

Be ready to share your thinking.
