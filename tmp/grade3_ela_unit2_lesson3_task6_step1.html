Subject-Verb Agreement

Listen and respond as your teacher explains subject-verb agreement, or how to make a subject and verb in a sentence agree.

Look at the following sentence:

Mary scrubs the bathtub.

  • What is the subject? What is the verb? Discuss with your teacher.

Next, look at the following sentences and choose the singular or plural verb in each:

  • Washing machines (is, are) useful.
  • The cousins (play, plays) under the oak tree.
  • John and Isaiah (read, reads) very well.
  • Discuss these sentences with your class.

Now work with a partner to choose the singular or plural verb for each of the following sentences:

  • The moon (is, are) bright.
  • Emma (eat, eats) a snack.
  • The baby (cry, cries).
