Day 2: Phonics Practice

  • Tell students the following:

    • Close your eyes and picture a car on the highway. You notice that a small trailer is hooked onto the back of the car. Now pretend the cars are words. The trailers or attachments are known as suffixes.

  • Ask if they have heard of suffixes and, if so, to name one or two of them. Explain that the suffixes they will focus on are -ed, -ing, -er, and -est.

  • Tell students that a suffix changes the meaning of the root or base word and sometimes changes the spelling as well.

  • Review the words with the students.

    • angrier
    • easiest
    • emptied
    • freezing
    • funniest
    • getting
    • greatest
    • heavier
    • leaving
    • pleased
    • shopped
    • strangest
    • swimming
    • using
    • worried

  • Now have them play “Root Word Detective.” Help them to remove the suffix and come up with the root or base word, if needed.

    Examples: angrier/angry; easiest/easy; emptied/empty, etc.
