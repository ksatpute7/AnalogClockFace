Day 3: Sort Game

  • Review the words with the students. In the review, focus on pattern and word meaning.

    • angrier
    • easiest
    • emptied
    • freezing
    • funniest
    • getting
    • greatest
    • heavier
    • leaving
    • pleased
    • shopped
    • strangest
    • swimming
    • using
    • worried

  • Remind students that on a previous day, they looked at words to see what makes them similar and what makes them different.

  • For today’s Sort Game, divide the class into four groups—one for each suffix. Assign a suffix and the spelling words with that suffix to each group.

    • -ed (emptied, pleased, shopped, worried)
    • -ing (freezing, getting, leaving, swimming, using)
    • -er (angrier, heavier)
    • -est (easiest, funniest, greatest, strangest)

  • Explain that the job of each group is to find the root or base word and then to clearly report to the class what happened to the base or root word when the suffix was added.

    For example, the group with the -ing suffix would have to explain that in the words freezing, leaving, and using, the base word dropped the e before adding -ing. The group would also need to report that in the words getting and swimming, the final consonant was doubled before adding -ing.

  • Elicit from each group a list of generalizations about what happens to base words when suffixes are added.

    • One generalization might be: Words that end in e drop the e before adding -ing. Capture these generalizations on a class chart.

  • Based on the results of this group work, decide whether students need further instruction with suffixes.

  • Consider telling the students about comparisons using -er and -est.
