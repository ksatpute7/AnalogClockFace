Day 2: Phonics Practice

  • Review the words with students.

    • basket
    • cactus
    • collar
    • happen
    • lesson
    • lettuce
    • napkin
    • puppet
    • sister
    • spelling
    • subject
    • suggest
    • supper
    • traffic
    • winter

  • Explain to students that when a word follows the VCCV pattern, it is divided into syllables between the consonants. Notice that the first syllable ends in a consonant. This is called a closed syllable. Closed syllables usually have a short vowel sound.

  • Show students how to highlight the VCCV pattern in the first five words. Then for the remainder of the spelling list, have students divide the words into syllables, read the syllables, and listen for the vowel sounds. Have them complete this process alone or with a partner.

  • Closely monitor students’ comprehension. If any need additional support, meet with them as a group to read one of the two decodable texts available for this phonics/spelling pattern.

  • Two decodable texts that address the weekly phonics skill or skills will be available to support groups of students. This week’s titles are A Winter Picnic and Summer Picnic.

  • Follow this suggested sequence when using the decodable books:

    • Have students read the title and preview the text.
    • Ask them to read the high frequency words.
    • Monitor students for reading and listen as they read. Have students read the entire story, switching readers after each page. Have partners reread the story. Make sure students are monitoring their reading.
    • Have students discuss the story, including the characters, setting, and events.
    • Encourage students to reread and record their reading, listening to be sure the reading is fluent.
