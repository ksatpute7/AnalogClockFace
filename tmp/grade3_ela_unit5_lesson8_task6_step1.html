What Your Story Says About You

Stories are fun to read. They can also teach you about the experiences and culture of the storyteller.

Listen as your teacher explains what that means.

  • Think about the story you told. What can people tell about you from the story you told?
  • Talk to a partner about what you think your audience would know about you from the stories you choose to tell.

Today, you are going to tell a new story, one that you create yourself. You will be thinking closely about what you want the audience to know about you and how you will weave that into your story.

Watch and listen as your teacher models how to consider the audience and add details to your storytelling to bring the audience in.

Talk to your partner about what you learned about your teacher from this story.
