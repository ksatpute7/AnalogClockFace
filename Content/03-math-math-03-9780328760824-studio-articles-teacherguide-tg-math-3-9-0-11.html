LESSON 11: Equal Perimeters

Lesson Overview

Students will explore the area of rectangles with the same perimeter. Students will complete the Self Check task.

Content Standards

3.MD—Measurement and Data

Geometric measurement: recognize perimeter as an attribute of plane figures and distinguish between linear and area measures.

  • 8. Solve real world and mathematical problems involving perimeters of polygons, including finding the perimeter given the side lengths, finding an unknown side length, and exhibiting rectangles with the same perimeter and different areas or with the same area and different perimeters.

Key Concepts

We have seen that area and perimeter are separate measurements. Rectangles can have the same perimeter but different areas.

One way to find perimeter is to add the length and width of the sides and double that sum. We can systematically find different rectangles with the same perimeter. For example; given a perimeter of 12 cm, the length and width will add to 6 (half of 12). Starting with a width of 1 cm, the area of rectangles with a perimeter of 12 can be found systematically:

1 cm×5 cm = 5  cm 2 2 cm×4 cm = 8  cm 2 3 cm×3 cm = 9  cm 2 4 cm×2 cm = 8  cm 2 5 cm×1 cm = 5  cm 2

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Find the area for different rectangles with the same perimeter.
