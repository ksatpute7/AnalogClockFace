Task 5: Reading Log

    • Discuss the importance of the Reading Log in developing the habit of reading and giving students a place where they will be able to reflect on the type of reading they are doing, how they are growing as a reader, and whether they are meeting their reading goals.

    • It is important for students to get into the routine of logging their daily reading in their Reading Log. By checking their entries periodically, you can see at a glance what and how much each student is reading. You can spot potential problems such as frequent abandonment of books, reading books that are too hard or too easy, not reading enough, etc. The Reading Log also provides potential grading possibilities.

    • Explain what a Reading Log is and why people keep them. If you are going to have students share their logs with each other, let them know that this is a way to find out about good books to read from people they know.

    • Help students set up a Reading Log, whether on paper or electronically. They should log the following information about each book:

      • Date
      • Book
      • Author
      • Pages Read
      • Comments, Questions, or Notes
      • 1 to 5 Stars Rating (when finished with the book)

    • Make sure to clarify your expectations about student entries to their Reading Log. You may choose to have the Reading Log used solely for Independent Reading books, or you may wish to have students also record their assigned class reading to help them stay in the habit of keeping their log up-to-date.

    • Have students add their homework reading choice to the log. Students can also log the text they read during Independent Reading in the prior lesson, if they like.
