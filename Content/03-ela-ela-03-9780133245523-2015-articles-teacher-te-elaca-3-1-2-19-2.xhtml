Task 2: Sentence Deconstruction

    • This task will help students understand how authors construct their sentences to make them more interesting. In this example, moving the clause to a different place in the sentence does not change the meaning.

    • Have them read the sentence. Then have them reread by reading the second clause first: You can understand more about your way of life today by learning about the history of your country and community.

    • Ask them if the meaning of the sentence changed as a result of placing the second part of the sentence first. (no)

      • Ask: Which way makes the meaning of the sentence clearer?

    • Then have students rearrange this sentence: When handling a hot pan, it is best to use a potholder.

    • Discuss how authors vary the construction of sentences to make their writing interesting and to vary the sentences in a paragraph.
