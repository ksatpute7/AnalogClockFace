Accurate, Dependable Information

Listen as your teacher reads “Wild or Wrangled.” Notice how your teacher points out examples of when the author uses accurate and dependable information.

When you read, you should be looking for clues in the text to let you know the information is dependable, such as photographs or experts. This text, “Wild or Wrangled,” is an example of how an author includes dependable sources in a text.
