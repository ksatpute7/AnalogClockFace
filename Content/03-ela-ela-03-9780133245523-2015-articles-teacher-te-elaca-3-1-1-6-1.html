Opening

Task 1: Phonics: Inflected Endings/Suffixes

    • Tell students that they are going to learn about inflectional endings or suffixes. Point out that some inflectional endings include -s, -es, -ed, and -ing, and the class is going to focus on -s and -es.

    • Tell students:

      • We combine the inflectional endings -s or -es with a root word to show that a word is plural. The inflection tells us something more about the root word. It tells us that it means more than one.

    • Explain that in some words, adding an inflectional ending also changes the number of syllables. Ask students to listen to how the following words with inflectional endings are pronounced: bunch/bunches, crash/crashes, wish/wishes, glass/glasses. Point out that in these words, the ending adds another syllable.

    • Display the following words and ask students to add -es and then pronounce the new word:

      church, bush, arch, class, dish, guess, box.
