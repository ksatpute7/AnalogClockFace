Task 9: Independent Reading

    • Go over the expectations of students during Independent Reading.

    • As students read, hold drop-in conferences with students about their reading.

      If there are struggling readers who cannot read the texts independently, make accommodations. However, as they become more familiar with the words and structures of each text, encourage them to give it a try without accommodations. Match the reader to the text that is of high interest to him/her or that you know is nearest the student’s reading level. Listen to each student read a few lines to make sure he or she has made the best choice.

    Decodables

    For students who might benefit from reading texts with more phonics support, use the decodable texts that are available.

    • This lesson’s titles are Frogs, Flies and Foxes and Jon and Jill.

    • Follow this suggested sequence when using the decodable books:

      • Have students read the high frequency words.
      • Have students read the title and preview the text.
      • Pair students for reading and listen as they read. Have students read the entire story, switching readers after each page. Have partners reread the story. Make sure students are monitoring their reading.
      • Have students discuss the story, including the characters, setting, and events.
      • Encourage students to reread and record their reading, listening to be sure the reading is fluent.
