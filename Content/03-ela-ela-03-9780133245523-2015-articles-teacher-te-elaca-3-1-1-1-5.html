Task 5: Grammar and Language: What Is a Noun?

    • Review the purpose of a noun. Tell students that things we can name are called nouns. Nouns are people, places, animals, things, or ideas.

    • Read aloud the following words: door, inside, cat, beautiful, oven, green, schoolyard, courage. Have students give a thumbs-up if the word is a noun and a thumbs-down if it isn’t. If necessary, remind students that courage is a noun because qualities and ideas are also nouns.

    • Remind students that nouns act as the subject of a sentence or as the object of a sentence. Display the following sentence: The girls played basketball. Point out that the subject of the sentence, which is in front of the verb, is girls. The word girls is a noun. Point out that the object of the sentence, or what the action is about, is basketball. Basketball is also a noun.

    • Have students use the annotation tools and the erase feature. Tell them they can use the erase feature to get rid of their highlighting if they make a mistake.

    • To provide some practice with this feature, ask students to highlight all six nouns in the three sentences: leaves, trees, tiger, kitten, students, trumpet. It may take them a few tries to get the hang of it.

    • Have students use the drawing feature to put an “S” over all of the subject nouns (leaves, tiger, and students) and an “O” over all the object nouns (trees, kitten, and trumpet).

    • Ask students to share their annotations with you.

    • Ask students to think about whether the word community is a noun. Tell students that during the next task they will take a first step at becoming a community of learners.
