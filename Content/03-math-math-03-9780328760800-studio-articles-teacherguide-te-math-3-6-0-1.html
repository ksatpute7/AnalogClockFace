LESSON 1: Multiplication Properties

Lesson Overview

Students continue the practice of chanting the multiplication chants to attain fluency. Then students do a card sort in which they match the multiplication properties they have previously learned with calculations and diagrams that make use of these properties. Finally, students use each of the properties they have learned so far to solve a single-digit multiplication problem.

Content Standards

3.OA—Operations and Algebraic Thinking

Solve problems involving the four operations, and identify and explain patterns in arithmetic.

9. Identify arithmetic patterns (including patterns in the addition table or multiplication table), and explain them using properties of operations. For example, observe that 4 times a number is always even, and explain why 4 times a number can be decomposed into two equal addends.

Key Concepts

While students are not expected to know the names of multiplication properties or their formal definitions yet, understanding and using the properties is an expectation, both in terms of third grade content and in terms of the mathematical practices. In informal—third grade appropriate—language we can state the multiplication properties students have experienced so far like this:

  • You can break the multiplier into parts to carry out the multiplication without changing the product.

  • You can break the multiplicand into parts to carry out the multiplication without changing the product.

  • If you increase (or decrease) the multiplier by 1, the product will increase by the multiplicand.

  • If you increase (or decrease) the multiplicand by 1, the product will increase by the multiplier.

  • If you switch the order of the multiplier and the multiplicand, the product does not change.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Match multiplication properties to calculations and diagrams that make use of them

  • Use multiplication properties to solve single-digit multiplication problems in multiple ways
