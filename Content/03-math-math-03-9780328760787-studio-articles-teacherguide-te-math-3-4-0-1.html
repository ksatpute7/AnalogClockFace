LESSON 1: What Is a Quadrilateral?

Lesson Overview

In this lesson, students explore quadrilaterals and their characteristics. They review the definition of a quadrilateral and they consider what characteristics define a shape.

Understand that shapes in different categories (e.g., rhombuses, rectangles, and others) may share attributes (e.g., having four sides), and that the shared attributes can define a larger category (e.g., quadrilaterals). Recognize rhombuses, rectangles, and squares as examples of quadrilaterals, and draw examples of quadrilaterals that do not belong to any of these subcategories.

Key Concepts

What are the attributes of quadrilaterals? Is it possible to make a quadrilateral with all sides the same length? What about with sides that are exactly two different lengths, or with each side a different length? Is it possible to make a quadrilateral with all angles the same size? What about a quadrilateral with angles that are exactly two different sizes or with each angle a different size? These are the questions students explore in this lesson. After exploring different quadrilaterals, students discuss the attributes of quadrilaterals and in the process review and refine the definitions of quadrilaterals they bring with them from second grade.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Review the definition of a quadrilateral.

  • Explore the attributes of quadrilaterals.
