LESSON 2: Rectangles

Lesson Overview

In this lesson, students investigate side lengths and angles of rectangles. They discover that rectangles have opposite sides that are the same length. Given one side, students will complete the rectangle. They will then draw rectangles to match a description.

Content Standards

3.G—Geometry

Reason with shapes and their attributes.

  • 1. Understand that shapes in different categories (e.g., rhombuses, rectangles, and others) may share attributes (e.g., having four sides), and that the shared attributes can define a larger category (e.g., quadrilaterals). Recognize rhombuses, rectangles, and squares as examples of quadrilaterals, and draw examples of quadrilaterals that do not belong to any of these subcategories.

Key Concepts

A rectangle has four right angles and opposites sides have the same length. Having opposite sides of the same length is an attribute of rectangles, but this attribute does not define the quadrilateral as a rectangle. (It could be any parallelogram.) However, a quadrilateral with four right angles must be a rectangle; therefore, the definition of a rectangle is a quadrilateral with four right angles.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Define a rectangle as a quadrilateral having four right angles.

  • Know that the opposite sides of rectangles are the same length.

Materials

  • Set squares (1 for each student)

  • Irregularly shaped paper—this could be any torn or cut-up scraps of construction paper (1 piece for each student)
