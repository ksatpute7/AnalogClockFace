LESSON 3: Which Operation?

Lesson Overview

In this problem-solving lesson, students will learn to use estimation and the relationship between inverse operations as tools to analyze computations for accuracy and reasonableness.

Content Standards

3.OA—Operations and Algebraic Thinking

Solve problems involving the four operations, and identify and explain patterns in arithmetic.

8. Solve two-step word problems using the four operations. Represent these problems using equations with a letter standing for the unknown quantity. Assess the reasonableness of answers using mental computation and estimation strategies including rounding.

Key Concepts

Students will begin the problem-solving process by making a diagram to represent a word problem. This will help them determine which operation is needed to solve the problem. Next, students make an estimate that will serve as a gauge for them to check the reasonableness of their solutions.

After students write an expression and find a solution, they use the inverse relationships between operations, which they discussed in Lessons 1 and 2, to check their solution. For example, if students solve a problem by multiplying 6 × 8 to get 48, then they can check the solution by dividing 48 by 6 to see if they get 8 or by dividing 48 by 8 to see if they get 6.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Estimate solutions to word problems

  • Solve word problems using all four operations

  • Check answers using inverse operations
