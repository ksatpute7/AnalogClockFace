LESSON 7: Putting It Together

Lesson Overview

Students review their work on the Self Check tasks and think about the feedback questions that you gave them. They will work with a partner to critique two samples of student work and to revise their work on the Self Check. Then students take a quiz to show how well they understand different kinds of quadrilaterals and how to find the area of quadrilaterals.

Content Standards

3.G—Geometry

Reason with shapes and their attributes.

1. Understand that shapes in different categories (e.g., rhombuses, rectangles, and others) may share attributes (e.g., having four sides), and that the shared attributes can define a larger category (e.g., quadrilaterals). Recognize rhombuses, rectangles, and squares as examples of quadrilaterals, and draw examples of quadrilaterals that do not belong to any of these subcategories.

3.MD—Measurement and Data

Geometric measurement: recognize perimeter as an attribute of plane figures and distinguish between linear and area measures.

8. Solve real world and mathematical problems involving perimeters of polygons, including finding the perimeter given the side lengths, finding an unknown side length, and exhibiting rectangles with the same perimeter and different areas or with the same area and different perimeters.

Key Concepts

Students have broadened their understanding of quadrilaterals and their attributes. They have also seen that these attributes can be common to more than one shape. The relationship of angles and side lengths is critical to understanding quadrilaterals:

  • A rectangle has four right angles and opposite sides that are the same length.

  • A square also has four right angles and four sides that are the same length.

  • A rhombus has four of each the same length. Its angles may or may not be right angles.

Students will use these attributes to critique two samples of student work and demonstrate their understanding of quadrilateral attributes.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Critique and revise student work.

  • Apply skills learned in the unit.

    • Understand definitions for quadrilaterals and the relationships between them.

    • Understand that some quadrilaterals can fit into more than one category.

    • Find the perimeter of quadrilaterals.
