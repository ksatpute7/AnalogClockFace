LESSON 2: Multiplying Ones and Tens

Lesson Overview

Students continue the practice of chanting the multiplication chants to attain fluency. Students consider what multiplying groups of ones means and they devise a rule for multiplying by 1. Then students consider what multiplying groups of tens means and they apply their understanding of place value to devising a rule for multiplying by 10.

Content Standards

3.OA—Operations and Algebraic Thinking

Multiply and divide within 100.

7. Fluently multiply and divide within 100, using strategies such as the relationship between multiplication and division (e.g., knowing that 8 × 5 = 40, one knows 40 × 5 = 8) or properties of operations. By the end of Grade 3, know from memory all products of two one-digit numbers.

3.NBT—Number and Operations in Base Ten

Use place value understanding and properties of operations to perform multi-digit arithmetic.

3. Multiply one-digit whole numbers by multiples of 10 in the range 10–90 (e.g., 9 × 80, 5 × 60) using strategies based on place value and the properties of operations.

Key Concepts

The identity property of multiplication states that the product of 1 and any number or variable is the number or variable itself.

Students focus on understanding why the product of any number and 1 is that number, and on understanding how to multiply 10 by a single-digit number.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Investigate groups of ones and write a rule for multiplying by 1

  • Construct the multiplication table for groups of ones

  • Investigate groups of tens and write a rule for multiplying by 10
