LESSONS 12–13: Gallery

Gallery Overview

Allow students who have a clear understanding of the content thus far in the unit to work on Gallery problems of their choosing. You can then use this time to provide additional help to students who need review of the unit's concepts or to assist students who may have fallen behind on work.

Gallery Descriptions

A Day at Dolores Huerta Elementary School

Using a series of clues, students will solve problems about the number of people at school.

Ms. Perkins's Fish

Students will use clues to figure out how many fish Ms. Perkins has in her three fish tanks.

A Plate of Cookies

Students will use a series of clues to figure out how many cookies a group of children ate.

Science Camp

Students will use a series of clues to figure out how many children went to Science Camp in August.

Car Wash

Students will help a group of students figure out how much money they must earn in a car wash to reach their goal.

Equation Match

Students will match story problems with the equations and models that represent them.

Going Shopping

Students will use diagrams and equations to solve some problems about shopping.

Ms. Perkins's Fish

Possible Answers

  1. Diagrams will vary.

    Tank 1: 14 Fish

    Tank 2: 14 + 9 = 23 Fish

    Tank 3: 14 + 23 = 37 Fish

    All three tanks combined: 14 + 23 + 37 = 74 fish

    Students may write a single equation: 14 + (14 + 9) + (14 + 14 + 9) = n. The parentheses are not needed, but students may use them to distinguish the three tanks.

A Plate of Cookies

Answers

  1. The parentheses are not needed, but students may use them to distinguish the different sets of cookies.

    (5 × 1) + (2 × 3) + (1 × 5) + (2 × n) = 28

    5 + 6 + 5 + 2 × n = 28

    16 + 2 × n = 28

    2 × n = 28 − 16

    2 × n = 12

    n = 6

    6 children ate 2 cookies.
