LESSON 7: Groups of 8 and 8 Groups

Lesson Overview

Students begin memorizing the eights by repeating a multiplication chant. Students use properties to solve a problem in two different ways.

Key Concepts

This lesson continues the use of the distributive property to decompose arrays into component parts. Multiplication problems can be decomposed by breaking down the multiplier, (a + b)c = ac + bc, or by breaking down the multiplicand, a(b + c) = ab + ac. The distributive property can also be used to break down both factors, decomposing the problem into four parts, (a + b)(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd.
