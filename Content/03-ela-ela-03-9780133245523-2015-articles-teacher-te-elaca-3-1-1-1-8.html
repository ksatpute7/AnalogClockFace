Task 8: Reader’s and Writer’s Share

    • Have students share their autobiographies with a partner and then with the rest of the class.

      Allow students with underdeveloped writing skills to work with a more advanced writer who can act as a peer mentor.

      Cultural Connection

      Some cultures do not reveal personal facts about themselves in such an open and immediate manner. Offer encouragement to ELLs who feel timid or who might be uncomfortable. Celebrate their uniqueness, as well as find commonalities in the group. This will assist those who originate from a more collectivist culture than an individualist culture. Do not force students to share their autobiography orally. Let them listen as the group shares and have them compare the autobiographies they hear to their own using silent reading.

    • Ask questions such as, “Do we have mystery lovers in our class? Readers who prefer nonfiction to fiction? Writers who love to write stories?”

    • This task is meant to get students excited about sharing their taste in books and writing.
