Task 4: Story Events

    • Add the temporal words and phrases that students identify to the chart you started earlier.

    • The purpose of this discussion is to critique Boxes for Katje as a quality narrative and also to remind students to use temporal words and phrases in their own narrative writing, just as author Candace Fleming did.

      Emerging P1.3.1

      Have Emerging ELLs work with a partner to describe the sequence of events in three steps using visuals and words like “first,” “second,” and “finally.” Students can revisit the Temporal Words chart for Boxes for Katje. Have students express ideas by answering yes-no and wh- questions. Help students understand why the author’s use of temporal words would help them understand Boxes for Katje.

      Expanding P1.3.1

      Have Expanding ELLs discuss the sequence of events in five steps: “first,” “second,” “next,” “after,” and “finally.”

      Have students contribute to the discussions by using the following sentence frames:

      • The author’s use of temporal words does/does not help me understand Boxes for Katje because _____.
      • Boxes for Katje is/is not a strong narrative because _____.

      Bridging P1.3.1

      Have Bridging ELLs describe with limited support the sequence of events and the temporal words found in Boxes for Katje. The students can use five steps to describe the sequence and to answer the questions with minimal prompting.

      Offer suggestions for students with disabilities to scaffold this activity. For example, they could create a graphic organizer or do a brainstorming exercise to generate ideas before the group discussion.
