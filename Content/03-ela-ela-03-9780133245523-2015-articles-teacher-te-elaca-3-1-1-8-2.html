Task 2: Temporal Words and Phrases

    • In this reading, you will use the text as a model to help students to understand the importance of the temporal words in noting the passage of time.

      Cultural Connection

      Ask ELLs to recall an important day in their lives (e.g., a special birthday, a celebration, or a very difficult day). They can Turn and Talk with another ELL to describe that day, explaining what time of day or night it started and two things they remember about it. Model an example:

      • I remember the day my sister was born. We woke up very early in the morning to go to the hospital. After she was born, my grandmother came over to stay with us to help. A few weeks later, she left. Now, my baby sister is five years old! Time has moved along!

    • Define the word temporal for students.

      Emerging P1.3.7

      For Emerging ELLs, define the word temporal. Select three students (any level) to come up to the front of the class and stand in a line. Orally state who is first in line, next in line, and last in line to depict order. Then, continue with examples in context. With prompting and substantial language support, have students identify temporal words and phrases in Boxes for Katje. Repeat these words with the students.

      Expanding P1.3.7

      Have Expanding ELLs define the word temporal, with prompting. Students can describe the language writers use to signal time changes with sentence frames:

      • Examples of temporal words and phrases are _____.
      • These words help the story to move along by _____.

      Bridging P1.3.7

      Have Bridging ELLs define the word temporal in their own words after seeing an example. Students can make their own list of temporal words from the text and can describe them with light support.

    • Have students discuss how they know time is passing in a text.
