Work Time

Task 8: Your School Community Discussion

    • Guidance for this task can be found in the Interview Preparation document.

    • Discuss the members of your school community.

    • List the roles of the community members: principal, assistant principal, classroom teacher, art teacher, music teacher, gym teacher, librarian, cafeteria worker, custodian, school nurse, security guard, counselor, etc. Post the list as a classroom reference.

      Cultural Connection

      Point out that these community members have their jobs because of their skills and talent. Have them guess which jobs require a college degree and which require training. Promote each job as equivalent in its importance (e.g., a custodian is as important to the school community as the principal is). Remind students that one goal of school is to help them prepare for college and to be ready for a career—whatever field it is. Prompt them to think about a career that they might like to have someday. Point out that they can be whatever they want by studying and working hard—no matter what their family income is and so on. Emphasize that everyone has the chance to go to college!

    • Talk about how the interview questions should include getting information on how the community member works with others in the school for the common good.

      Emerging P1.3.1

      Emerging ELLs can benefit from interviewing adults in familiar roles. This will allow students to have the background knowledge and the vocabulary necessary to write, discuss, and present about this individual. Assigning students to interview a classroom teacher, an art teacher, a music teacher, or an ESL teacher will allow them to feel more comfortable during the interview process.

      Expanding P1.3.1

      Pair Expanding ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency. Carefully think about what person each partnership will interview. Expanding ELLs can benefit from interviewing adults in familiar roles since ELLs will have the background knowledge and vocabulary to write, discuss, and present about this individual.

      Bridging P1.3.1

      Pair Bridging ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency or with a strong ELA student who is English proficient. These students can be assigned school members whose roles require vocabulary that is more complex.

    • Use the list of community members you created to allow partners to select one member of the school community to interview. Note on the chart which partners will interview each community member. The goal is to have each of the roles of the school community represented rather than having multiple partners interview the same person. If needed, interview a classroom teacher at each grade level.

    • Students will interview their assigned community member in the next lesson via email.
