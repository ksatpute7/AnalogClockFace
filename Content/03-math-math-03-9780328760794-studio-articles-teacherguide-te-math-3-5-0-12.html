LESSONS 12-14: Gallery

Gallery Overview

Allow students who have a clear understanding of the content thus far in the unit to work on Gallery problems of their choosing. You can then use this time to provide additional help to students who need review of the unit's concepts or to assist students who may have fallen behind on work.

Gallery Descriptions

Abdul Learns to Multiply

Students will help Abdul learn how to use the facts he already knows to solve a multiplication fact he does not know.


Helping Kai

Students will help Kai solve a multiplication problem using facts he already knows.


The Bakery

How many muffins did the baker sell? Students will find the number of muffins sold by looking at how many muffins are left.


Jasper and Chan

Students will determine how many more oranges are in 8 groups of 9 than in 6 groups of 9.


True or False?

Are the equations true or false? Without doing any calculations, students will use what they know about multiplication to decide which equations are true and which are false.


What Is My Perimeter?

Students will find the perimeter of each figure when given the length of one side. All sides have the same length in each figure.


A Plate of Cookies

Students will use the information in a math story to figure out how many children ate 2 cookies.


A Box of Chocolates

How many chocolates are left in the box? Students will calculate the number of remaining chocolates in two different ways.


Trading Cards

How many trading cards does Evan have? Students will find the total number of trading cards in Evan’s albums.

Abdul Learns to Multiply

Possible Answers

  1. Abdul knows how to find  9×5 because 9×5=5×9 . If you turn a 9-by-5 array around, it becomes a 5-by-9 array. The total number of things in the array does not change.

    Abdul could also calculate this problem by breaking up the problem into (4×5)+(5×5) .

Helping Kai

Possible Answers

Answers will vary.

  1. Because Kai knows the twos and the fives facts, he can change 7×8 into (5×8)+(2×8)=40+16=56 .

    Because Kai also knows the threes and fours facts, he can also change 7×8 into (3×8)+(4×8)=24+32=56 .

The Bakery

Answers

  1. The baker sold 54 muffins.

  2. A full tray has 6×5=30 muffins. To calculate how many muffins have sold, determine the array size of the sold (missing) muffins.

    The first tray sold 0 muffins

    The second tray sold  4×4=16 muffins.

    The third tray sold 4×5=20  muffins.

    The fourth tray sold 6×3=18  muffins.

    16+20+18=54

Jasper and Chan

Answers

  1. Jasper has 18 more oranges.
  2. Jasper has 6×9 oranges and Chan has 8×9 oranges. Since Chan has 8−6=2 more boxes than Jasper and each box has 9 oranges, she has 2×9=18 more oranges.

A Plate of Cookies

Answers

  1. Six children each ate 2 cookies.

  2. If each of 5 children ate 1 cookie, 5 cookies got eaten, because 5×1=5 .

    If each of 2 children ate 3 cookies, 6 cookies got eaten, because 2×3=6 .

    If 1 child ate 5 cookies, 5 cookies got eaten, because 1×5=5 .

    These 7 children ate 16 cookies, 5+6+5=16 .

    The rest of the children ate 12 cookies, because 28−16=12 .

    Because each of them ate 2 cookies, there were 6 children, because 6×2=12 .

A Box of Chocolates

Possible Answers

Answers will vary.

  1. One way.

    (2×4)+(4×8)=8+32=40

    There are 40 chocolates in the box.

  2. Another way.

    The box has room for 6 rows of 8, but 2 rows of 4 are missing.

    Move 4 chocolates to make 5 rows of 8.

    (6×8)−(2×4)=48−8=40

    There are 40 chocolates in the box.

Trading Cards

Answers

  1. The first album holds 4×6=24 cards.

    The second album holds 6×8=48 cards.

    Evan has 24+48=72 cards altogether.
