Key Concepts

A fraction is a number that names a part of the whole. For example, 1 6 MathType@MTEF@5@5@+= feaagKart1ev2aqatCvAUfKttLearuWrPXgBGmvyUnhij5MzH5gDGm fDKbqeduuDJXwAKbYu51MyVXgaruqr1ngBPrgatCvAUfeBSjuyZL2y d9gzLbvyNv2CaeHbd9wDYLwzYbItLDharyavP1wzZbItLDhis9wBH5 garqqtubsr4rNCHbGeaGqiFC0de9sqaqpepC0xbbL8F4rqqrFfpeea 0xe9Lq=Jc9vqaqpepm0xbba9pwe9Q8fs0=yqaqpepae9pg0Firpepe KkFr0xfr=xfr=xb9adbaqaaeGaciGaaiaadeqaaqaafaGbaaGcbaWa aSaaaeaacqaIXaqmaeaacqaI0aanaaaaaa@4586@ is a fraction. The denominator, 6, tells the number of equal parts that 1 has been divided into. The numerator, 1, tells how many of the equal parts you have. You can represent fractions using number lines and area models.
