Task 3: Temporal Words in Boxes for Katje

    • Divide students into several groups and assign pages to each group for rereading Boxes for Katje. After this rereading, students will use the text as a model for the writing they are about to try on their own.

    • Direct students to annotate (highlight) the temporal words and phrases they find in Boxes for Katje. You might choose to annotate the first few paragraphs with the students if they need the support of a teacher model.

      Model multiple ways to annotate the text (e.g., highlighting, underlining, color-coding different types of information) to allow student choice in selecting the method that allows them to best demonstrate their knowledge.

      Emerging P1.3.7

      Group Emerging ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency to help explain the concept of temporal words and phrases. Include students who speak the native language of the ELLs as well. Students should be able to locate at least two to three temporal words in the text.

      Expanding P1.3.7

      Provide Expanding ELLs with hints as to the temporal words and phrases. Tell them that they are looking for words like “then,” “week,” and “morning.” Students should be able to locate at least four to five temporal words.

      Bridging P1.3.7

      Encourage Bridging ELLs to keep reading to find the temporal words and phrases. They may need to go over their assigned pages more closely after a first reread. Students should be able to locate at least five temporal words and phrases.
