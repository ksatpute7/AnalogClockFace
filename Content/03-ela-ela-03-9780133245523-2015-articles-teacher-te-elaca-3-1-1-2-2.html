Task 2: The Five-Finger Rule

    • Tell students that one way to become a stronger reader is to read books that are just right: not too hard, but not too easy. Ask students:

      • How would you choose a just-right book?

    • Explain that there is a quick way to do this. It’s called the Five-Finger Rule and it can give students further direction when choosing a book. Consider printing or making a chart or poster of the Five-Finger rule so that students can see it all year while choosing books.

      The Five-Finger Rule helps you decide if you have a just-right book. Here’s what you do:

      • Choose a book and read a page from the middle of the book.
      • Put one finger up for every word you either can’t pronounce or don’t know the meaning of.
      • If five of your fingers go up while reading, choose another book.
      • If only two or three fingers go up, you’ve found a just-right book.
