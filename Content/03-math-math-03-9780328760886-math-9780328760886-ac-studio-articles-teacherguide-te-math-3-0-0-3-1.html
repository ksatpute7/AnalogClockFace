Task 1: Patterns in the Hundred Chart

Lesson Guide

Project the Hundred Chart tool, which is available in the Notebook.

Have students describe the patterns they see, and explain why they think each pattern appears the way it does. Some patterns that students should notice include the following.

  • Numbers in a column all have the same digit in the ones place.

  • Numbers in a row all have the same digit in the tens place, except for in the last column. The digit in the tens place in the last column is 1 more than the digit in the tens place in the rest of the row because when you count up to 10 you make a new ten.

  • As you move to the right, the digit in the ones place increases by 1 until you get to the last column. The digit in the ones place in the last column in every row is 0, because you made a new ten. Conversely, when you move to the left, the digit in the ones place decreases by 1 (again, except for the number to the left of the last column). Check that students see that as they move to the right through the chart, they count by 1s.

  • As you move down, the digit in the tens place increases by 1. Conversely, as you move up, the digit in the tens place decreases by 1.

  • The numbers you say when you count by 10s are all in the last column.

  • The numbers you say when you count by 5s are all in two columns: the column with 5 at the top and the column with 10 at the top.

  • The even numbers are in every other column (in the columns with 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10 at the top).

SWD:

Tap on the parts of the hundred chart that you or a student refers to so that the shading will help students follow along more easily.
