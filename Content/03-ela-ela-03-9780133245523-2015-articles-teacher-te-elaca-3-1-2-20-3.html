Task 3: Interview Report Share

    • Have students share their interview report with a partner.

      Emerging P1.3.9

      Allow Emerging ELLs to practice their oral presentation skills by reading their report aloud to an adult or to an English-proficient student who can aid with pronunciation prior to reading their report to a peer. Encourage students to demonstrate active listening skills by asking their partner a basic question after hearing their interview report.

      Expanding P1.3.9

      Allow Expanding ELLs to practice reading their report aloud to themselves several times prior to presenting it to their peer. Encourage students to demonstrate active listening skills by asking their partner a detailed question after listening to their interview report.

      Bridging P1.3.9

      Allow Bridging ELLs to practice reading their report aloud to themselves once prior to presenting it to their peer. Encourage students to demonstrate active listening skills by asking their partner a detailed question after listening to their interview report.

    • Consider doing a Gallery Walk where students walk around the classroom and read each other’s reports.

    • Use students’ interview reports to assess the students’ writing strengths in this genre. Evaluate the work by using the Grade 3 Informational Writing Rubric. Record strengths, areas of need, and mechanics/conventions that need attention.

      Differentiate the assessment of SWDs as needed. Identify specific aspects of the rubric that correspond with their individual learning goals to prioritize during this task. Allow your prioritization to be reflected in the grading. Alternatively, allow students to make corrections to conventions (i.e., capitalization, punctuation, and spelling) before grading.
