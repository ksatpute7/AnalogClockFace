Adding Suffixes

Most root words don’t change their spelling when you add a suffix. Listen as your teacher discusses exceptions to this rule.

Use what you know to add the suffix -ed to the following words.

  • surprise
  • supply
  • trust
  • whip
  • slap
  • amuse
  • blot
  • enjoy

The following are the rules that usually apply when the main rule doesn’t.

When a word ends in silent e:

  • Drop the e if you are adding a suffix that begins with a vowel, but keep the e when you are adding a suffix that begins with a consonant.
  • Examples: decode-decodable, love-loveless.

When a word ends in y:

  • Change the y to i when you are adding a suffix if the letter before the y is a consonant, but do not change the spelling if the letter before the y is a vowel.
  • Examples: study-studied, destroy-destroyed.

When a one-syllable word ends in the CVC combination:

  • Double the final consonant when adding a suffix that begins with a vowel, but not if the suffix begins with a consonant. If you don’t double the final consonant, the first vowel sound will change from a short sound to a long sound. For example, if you add -ed to ship to make it shiped, it would be pronounced with a long i sound to rhyme with swiped. But that’s not right!
  • Examples: ship-shipped, ship-shipment
