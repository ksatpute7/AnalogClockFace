LESSON 3: Groups of 6 and 6 Groups

Lesson Overview

Students begin memorizing the sixes by repeating a multiplication chant. They work with arrays to understand that 6 x n = n x 6.

Key Concepts

To examine the commutative property of multiplication, it is useful to have a symmetrical situation. Because rectangular arrays give us such a situation, they are particularly useful models for exploring the commutative property of multiplication. Notice that when the 4 by 6 array below is turned sideways, it becomes a 6 by 4 array. No objects are added or removed when the array is turned. The product, 24, is unchanged.

The basket of apples in this lesson is an array that is purposely rotated so that students may more easily visualize that if the basket is turned one way, it contains 6 rows of 4 apples and if it is turned the other way, it contains 4 rows of 6 apples. Either way, it contains 24 apples.
