LESSON 8: Putting it Together

Lesson Overview

Students measure the weights and volumes of teacups and coffee mugs and solve problems based on their measurements. Students also review and revise their work on the Self Check tasks from the previous lesson.

Key Concepts

Numeric values are determined through making correct measurements. These numbers in turn are used to solve problems involving addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Measure volume and weight.

  • Solve problems involving grams, kilograms, liters, and milliliters.

  • Review and revise the Self Check tasks.

Materials

  • Teacups and coffee mugs (one of each for each pair of students; see Preparation)

  • Pan balance and weights (one for each pair of students)

  • Liter cube (one for each pair of students)

  • Source of water for measuring volume

Preparation

Collect a variety of teacups and coffee mugs and place them in a public area. These containers are ideal for this activity because they generally weigh less than 1/2 kg and hold less than 1 L.
