LESSON 9: Project Presentations

Lesson Overview

Students will present their projects and receive feedback from their classmates and teacher. Students will evaluate other students' presentations.

Content Standards

3.MD—Measurement and Data

  • 3. Draw a scaled picture graph and a scaled bar graph to represent a data set with several categories. Solve one- and two-step “how many more” and “how many less” problems using information presented in scaled bar graphs. For example, draw a bar graph in which each square in the bar graph might represent 5 pets.

  • 4. Generate measurement data by measuring lengths using rulers marked with halves and fourths of an inch. Show the data by making a line plot, where the horizontal scale is marked off in appropriate units— whole numbers, halves, or quarters.

Key Concepts

Given a set of data, what is the most appropriate graph to use? Is there more than one way to display the data? Part of graphing and statistics is deciding the purpose of the graph and the best way to display data to make your conclusions clear.

There are several decisions to be made:

  • type of graph to use

  • the appropriate scale

  • value of the icon

As this thinking develops, there is also the realization that data and graphs can be manipulated to show, or not show, what is desired. For example, scale can be used to emphasize a difference in categories, or to make the difference appear minimal. Is the purpose of the graph to show data objectively or subjectively?

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Present projects and demonstrate an understanding of graphing data

  • Understand, describe, and interpret graphs
