Text Structure

Read the table of contents in Turtles & Tortoises. The table of contents page can help you predict how a book might be organized or structured. It is helpful to think about what you know and expect to find before you begin reading. This is called “activating background knowledge.” This helps you pull together information before you read to help you better understand what you read.

Follow along as your teacher reads aloud Turtles & Tortoises. Listen as your teacher points out text structures.

Like your own poster, Turtles & Tortoises presents information about an animal, discusses problems with this animal in communities, and offers helpful solutions.

You may want to use some of the text structures you see in Turtles & Tortoises in your poster project.
