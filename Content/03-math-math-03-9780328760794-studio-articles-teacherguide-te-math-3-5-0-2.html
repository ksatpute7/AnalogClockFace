LESSON 2: The Sixes

Lesson Overview

Students review the facts learned in Unit 3.3 by repeating a multiplication chant. Students use the patterns they noticed in Lesson 1 to build the Sixes Multiplication Table. They use properties to solve a problem in two different ways.

Key Concepts

In Unit 3.3 the focus was on building and replicating groups in order to help students understand the specific meanings of each factor. In this unit, the focus is on discerning the structure and using the properties in order to build the multiplication tables.

The commutative property—ab = ba—can be most easily seen by rotating an array to show that when the array is turned, the rows become columns and the columns become rows.

The distributive property—a(b + c) = ab + ac—can be seen in the multiplication table. The sum of the product in the cells in the a and b rows always equals the product in the a + b row.

The associative property—(a x b) x c = a x (b x c)—can’t be visualized as easily as the distributive and commutative properties, but can be found by reasoning. For example, I know that 8 = 2 x 4. To calculate 8 x 7, 1 can calculate 4 x 7 and double that.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Investigate groups of 6

  • Use patterns based on properties to complete the Sixes Multiplication Table

  • Identify patterns in the multiples of 6
