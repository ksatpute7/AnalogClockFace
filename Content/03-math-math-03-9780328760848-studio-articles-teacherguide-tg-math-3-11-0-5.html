LESSON 5: Measuring Weight

Lesson Overview

Students watch a video in which nonstandard units (i.e., nickels and dimes) are used to weigh an object; they learn the importance of using standardized measurement units. Students estimate and then measure the weight of various objects in grams and kilograms using a pan balance.

Key Concepts

This lesson helps students internalize the relative magnitude of grams to kilograms.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Develop an internal sense of grams and kilograms by estimating and then measuring weight.

  • Use a pan balance to measure the weight of objects in grams and kilograms.

Materials

  • Pan balance and weights (one for each group of four students)

  • Selection of objects ranging in weight from 1 g to 2.5 kg, depending on the capacity of your pan balances and on the masses you have available (enough for each group of four students to select five objects)

Preparation

Calibrate each of the pan balances. Place a pan balance and set of weights on each group's work area.
