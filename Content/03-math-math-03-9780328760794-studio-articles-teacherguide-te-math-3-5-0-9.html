LESSON 9: Groups of 9 and 9 Groups

Lesson Overview

Students begin memorizing the nines by repeating a multiplication chant. They use properties to solve a problem in two different ways.

Key Concepts

Seeing that changing the order of the factors in a multiplication does not change the product is straightforward in the symmetrical context of arrays. Turn a 6 x 9 array around and you have changed it into a 9 x 6 array without changing the total number of objects.

Seeing that order does not matter is not as easy in a context of linear measures. Here students are presented with a tape diagram of 6 lengths of 9 and asked how many lengths of 6 are needed to make the same total length.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Begin memorizing the nines facts

  • Use arrays to confirm that reversing the order of factors does not change the product

  • Use arrays to investigate the distributive property
