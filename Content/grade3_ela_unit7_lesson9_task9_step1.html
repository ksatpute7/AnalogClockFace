The Tale of Despereaux

First, your teacher will introduce the Main Idea Thinking Guide Form, a tool that helps you understand what you are reading.

Then, begin to read The Tale of Despereaux by Kate DiCamillo. You may read this independently or with a partner.

You should read Chapters 1–18 by Lesson 11.

At the end of Chapter 3 in the text, it says, “Reader, you must know that an interesting fate (sometimes involving rats, sometimes not) awaits almost everyone, mouse or man, who does not conform.”

Jot down your responses to these questions in your Notebook.

  • What does this mean? How do you think this applies to Despereaux? How does this apply to communities?
  • Why do authors use animals as characters?
  • What can humans learn by studying animals and their behavior?
  • Animals, both wild and domestic, are part of our communities. What responsibility do humans have toward animals?
