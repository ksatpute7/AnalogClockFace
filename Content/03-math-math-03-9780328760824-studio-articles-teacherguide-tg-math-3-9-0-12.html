LESSON 12: Putting It Together

Lesson Overview

Students will review their work on the Self Check task. Then students will critique samples of student work. Finally, students will use what they learned by critiquing student work to revise their work on the Self Check task.

Key Concepts

Area is measured in square units, while perimeter is measured in linear units. Area of a rectangle is found by counting the number of square units or by multiplying the length by the width. Rectilinear figures can be divided into rectangular parts whose areas can be found and added. Perimeter can be found for a rectangle if the length and width are given. Different rectangles can have the same area but different perimeters or the same perimeter but different areas.

Each of the pieces of work students critique exhibits some misunderstanding or confusion about area and perimeter. A good critique of each piece should do these things:

  • Say what, if anything, is done well

  • Say what errors are in the work

  • Correct the work

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Review and revise Self Check.

  • Critique samples of student work.

  • Consolidate understanding of area and perimeter.
