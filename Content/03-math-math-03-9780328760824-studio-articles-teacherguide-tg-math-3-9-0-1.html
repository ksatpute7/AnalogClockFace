LESSON 1: Which Covers More Area?

Lesson Overview

Students will begin to explore the idea of area as a measurement by comparing the area of two figures.

Key Concepts

Area is a measure of the size of two-dimensional figures.

The area of two figures can be compared directly by placing one figure on top of the other. This is a simple process if the figures are proportional, but becomes more complicated if the figures are not proportional. In this case the need for a standard unit for measuring area becomes clear: how many more area units does one figure have than the other? Even when comparing proportional figures there is still the question of how much larger one figure is than the other.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Define area.

  • Compare the area of two rectangles using direct comparison.
