Task 5: Partner Discussion

    • Partner discussion, or Turn and Talk, gives you a chance to listen to the conversations of students to see if they understand the ideas in the lesson.

    • Review what the word retelling means. Model a retelling for the students using a familiar fairytale or myth.

    • In your retelling, use phrases such as “first this happened … next this happened.” Point out that you are not adding any information that wasn’t already in the story.

    • Now have students work with a partner to retell Boxes for Katje. They can return to the text as necessary.

    • Retelling a story orally with a partner is a way to help students understand narrative structure.

      Provide students with a model retelling to use as a reference as they work. Post the phrases “first this happened … next this happened …”

      Emerging P1.3.9

      Show Emerging ELLs how to use their fingers for a retelling. Hold up your thumb and say, “First, this happened….” Each of the next three fingers is “Next, this happened….” Finally, for the pinky (or fifth finger) finger, say, “Last, this happened….”

      Students should take a Picture Walk of the story to help them retell it. They should be able to point at the pictures to aid in the retelling.

      Expanding P1.3.9

      Have Expanding ELLs plan their retelling with a partner. Students can use the sentence frames to aid in the retelling:

      • First, this happened _____.
      • Next, this happened _____.
      • Last, this happened _____.

      Bridging P1.3.9

      Have Bridging ELLs refer back to details from the text to help retell the story. This will help students deliver longer oral presentations. Students can use specific details from the text and not just their memory.

    • Observe students to see if they are following the etiquette of Classroom Conversations.
