Work Time

Task 5: Vocabulary

    • Lead students in discussing the following words from the text: contribute, enforces, varies.

      • Read aloud this sentence from What is a Community? from A to Z: “Shelter, food, clothing, and community services contribute to the quality of life.”
        • Tell students: When you contribute something, you give or add to it, or make it better. You contribute to your soccer team when you play hard. You contribute to a garden project when you plant flowers.

          Have students say one thing they contribute to.
      • Read aloud this sentence from What is a Community? from A to Z: “The municipal government enforces the laws in a town or city.”
        • Ask students: Why does a government need to enforce the laws? (to make sure people follow them; to keep people safe)

          Have students say in their own words what they think enforce means.
      • Say the following:
        • Imagine you bought ice cream from a store. Then you bought ice cream from another store. The second ice cream was not as good as the first. You might say the taste of the ice cream varies from store to store.

          Then have students say in their own words what they think varies means.

    • Read aloud this sentence from the Common Read: “Quality of life varies from one community to another.” Have students use their understanding of the word varies to say what they think the sentence means.

    • Remind students to enter the three words into their Personal Notebook along with a short definition for each word.
