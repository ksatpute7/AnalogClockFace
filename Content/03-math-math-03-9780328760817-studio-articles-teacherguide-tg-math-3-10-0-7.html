LESSON 7: Project Work Day and Gallery

Project Work Day and Gallery Overview

Have students work to complete their projects. If any students complete the project before the end of class, allow them to work on Gallery problems of their choosing. You can then use this time to provide additional help to students who need review of the unit's concepts or to assist students who may have fallen behind on work.

Gallery Descriptions

  1. Work on Your Project 53

    Students will work with their partner and prepare for their presentation. If students are creating bar graphs, they may need to create one on paper or in their Notebooks to control the vertical axis scale intervals. Students who are using a line plot will need to make the line plot graph on paper or in the Notebooks to meet the one fourth interval requirement on the horizontal axis.

  2. Petting Zoo 53

    Students will read a bar graph and answer questions.

  3. Book Graph 54

    Students will read a picture graph and answer questions.

  4. Handfuls 54

    Students will read a line plot and answer questions.

  5. Ice Cream Shop 54

    Students will find the scale for a picture graph.

  6. Birds at The Feeder 55

    Students will complete a bar graph.

  7. Buying Hats 55

    Students will read a bar graph and help students buy hats for a class trip.

Work on Your Project

Work with your partner to review the project rubric.

  • Take a few minutes to study the rubric by yourself.

  • Without looking at the rubric, take 1 minute to describe the rubric as completely as possible to your partner (who can look at the rubric). Your partner should listen carefully to your description.

Work on your project with your partner.

  • Complete the graph, making sure all labels and the title are included.

  • Make a list of five questions to ask the class about your graph, along with the answers.

  • Discuss how you will present your project

  • Share the topic you chose and why, and the title of your graph.

  • Show your graph and explain what it shows, and what you concluded about your topic.

Book Graph

Answers

  1. Mrs. Adams's class read 90 books. There are 9 icons for Mrs. Adams's class; each icon represents reading 10 books.
    9×10=90

  2. Mr. Makris's class read about 85 books; Ms. Lee's class read 70 books. Mr. Makris's class read 15 more books than Ms. Lee's class.
    85−15=70

  3. The classes read 245 books. Mrs. Adams's class read 90; Mr. Makris's class read 85; Ms. Lee's class read 70.
    90+85+70=245

Handfuls

Answers

  1. 5 students picked up 10 cubes.

  2. 12 cubes each were picked up by the 7 students who picked up the same number of cubes.

  3. 11 students in the class picked up 10 or fewer cubes each.

  4. More students picked up an even number of cubes. 15 students picked up even numbers of cubes, 12 students picked up odd numbers of cubes. 3 more students picked up an even number of cubes.
    15−12=3

  5. 19 is an outlier. Perhaps the student miscounted or the student has really large hands.

Birds at the Feeder

Answers

  1. 5 chickadees visited the feeder.

  2. 28 birds visited the feeder. Of these 7 were gold finches, 14 were house finches, and 2 were downy woodpeckers, 23 birds in total. So, there had to be 5 chickadees for a total of 28 birds.

  3. Abdul saw gold finches half as often as he saw house finches. He saw house finches 14 times and gold finches 7 times.
