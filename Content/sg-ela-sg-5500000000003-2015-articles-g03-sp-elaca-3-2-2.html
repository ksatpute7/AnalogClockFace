Day 2: Closed Sort

  • Review the words with students. In the review, focus on pattern and word meaning.

    • bodies
    • bunches
    • copies
    • crashes
    • families
    • glasses
    • inches
    • lists
    • parties
    • pencils
    • pennies
    • plants
    • pockets
    • supplies
    • wishes

  • Remind students that on a previous day they looked at words to see what makes them similar and what makes them different.

  • Help students identify the root words. Then ask students if they see any patterns in how the plural was formed. Elicit from students generalizations such as the following:

    • For words ending in -ch or -sh or -ss, add -es to make the plural.
    • For words ending in -y, change the -y to -i and add -es (body, copy, family, party, penny, supply).
    • For all other words, add -s to make them plural.

  • Have students complete the sort with the remainder of the words.

  • Review the results of the sort. Ask students to explain why they made the specific choices, or have them make a generalization about the words in each category.

  • Share the spelling pattern for each ending with the class. Have students add other words that follow these patterns in their Spelling Notebook.
