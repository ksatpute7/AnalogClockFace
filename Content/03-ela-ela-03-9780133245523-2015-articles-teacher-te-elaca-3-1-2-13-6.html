Task 6: Partner Work: P and R Gist

    • This task will give students more practice in writing Gists. Students will work with partners to write one Gist for two pages.

    • First, have partners choose who is going to write a Gist for the P page and who is going to write a Gist for the R page. Remind students that their Gists should be no more than 20 words. Then instruct students to write their Gists.

    • Next, have partners share their Gists with each other. Then instruct them to combine the ideas in their Gists to write one Gist together. This Gist should summarize both the P and R pages.

    • Monitor students as they write and offer guidance and support.

      Emerging P1.3.10

      Emerging ELLs can benefit from joint writing the Gists for P and R together in a small group. Provide students with 20 spaces that they can fill in as a group to create the Gist. Students can orally state the Gists with their group following turn-taking protocols. Support students through prompting and answering of wh- questions. Students in the small group can then combine the Gists to create one that summarizes both pages.

      Expanding P1.3.10

      Expanding ELLs can benefit from joint writing the Gists for P and R with a partner. Together the partnership can combine the Gists to create one for both pages. Encourage students to paraphrase the text using key vocabulary and complete sentences.

      Bridging P1.3.10

      Pair Bridging ELLs with a non-ELL peer. Allow partnerships to write the Gists jointly for each page following turn-taking protocols. Encourage students to include academic vocabulary and complex and compound sentences in their 20-word Gist.
