Task 3: Parts of an Email

    • Review the parts of an email:

      • heading, which contains the recipient’s address
      • subject, which says what the email is about
      • body of the message, which explains what the sender is asking or telling
      • signature, which contains the sender’s name and contact information

    • Explain that students will write the message in their Notebook, share their message with you, and then you will send the email to their community member.

    • Display a model message and discuss how to write the greeting and closing of the message.

    • As a group, create an introductory paragraph that will be included in the emails to the community members. This paragraph should provide a description of the project (a study of community). Consult Introductory Interview Email on the Teacher Support site for help with this.

    • Share the introductory paragraph with the students. They should include this in their message to the community member they are interviewing.

    • Though students will not actually be sending the emails, stress the importance of rereading an email before pressing the “send” button.
