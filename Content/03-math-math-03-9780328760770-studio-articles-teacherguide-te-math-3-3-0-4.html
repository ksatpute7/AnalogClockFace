LESSON 4: Groups of Five

Lesson Overview

Students begin memorizing the fives facts by repeating a multiplication chant. Next, students learn how to use the Tape Diagram tool to model multiplication situations. They use the Tape Diagram tool to work with multiplication in the context of equal lengths of craft sticks. They consider what happens to the total length if another 5-inch length is added. Finally, students look for structure in the Fives Multiplication Table.

Key Concepts

Multiplication can be used for more than finding the total number of objects in equal groups of objects. While many uses of multiplication will not be taught until the fourth or fifth grade, third-grade students can consider multiplication situations in which the multiplicand is a length rather than a collection of objects. Length is used here to continue developing students’ understanding that the multiplicand is a unit that can be counted. The length 5 inches can be counted; 5 inches can be a group.

Goals and Learning Objectives
