LESSON 2: How Many for Each Person?

Lesson Overview

Students chant along with the multiplication animations to attain mastery of the threes. Then they solve a division problem that involves equal shares of countable objects. They prepare a presentation that includes a model of the problem, an equation, and an answer to the problem.

Key Concepts

In equal sharing, or partitive situations, the number of groups is known and we divide to find the number in each group. In this lesson, students divide countable objects to make equal shares.

This is a typical equal-shares math story:

  • There are 12 cookies. Mrs. Anderson wants to pack them equally into her four children's lunch bags. How many cookies should she put in each bag?

Acting out this problem might involve an action much like dealing cards. The diagram below shows how one might distribute 12 cookies into 4 groups. The numbers on the cookies indicate the order in which they were dealt out.

The total and the number of groups are known. The problem asks how many are in each group. That question can be answered by asking, “Four times what number makes 12 ( 4×☐=12 )?”
