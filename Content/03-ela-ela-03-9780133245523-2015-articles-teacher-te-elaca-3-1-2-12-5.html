Task 5: Community Discussion

    • Listen in as students share their writing and talk about whether your school is a community. If needed, ask students questions to help them correct any misconceptions. You might ask:

      • How does the text define community?
      • Is our school a place where people share goods, services, and buildings?
      • Does our school have customs and ideas that we share?
      • Do people in our school have different jobs?
      • Does anything in the text about kinds of community help us decide if the school is a community?

      Validate students’ ideas while listening in as they share with a partner. This will encourage them to share in the Whole Group Discussion. Let them know what they could share and that you will call on them. It may be helpful to have students write down what they plan to share in the discussion. This way they need not rely on memory when participating in the class discussion.

      Emerging P1.3.3

      Prior to the Whole Group Discussion, activate Emerging ELLs’ prior knowledge by referring to the list on communities the class created in the previous lesson. Allow students to use their Personal Notebook to review academic vocabulary, and prompt them to use these words when sharing their ideas. Encourage ELLs to participate in the discussion by asking students yes-no questions and having them express their ideas in short sentences.

      Expanding P1.3.3

      Expanding ELLs can benefit from being able to reference the class list on communities throughout the Whole Group Discussion. Allow students to use their Personal Notebook to review academic vocabulary, and prompt them to use these words when sharing their ideas. Encourage Expanding ELLs to contribute to the discussion by engaging in sustained dialogue with complete sentences and adding relevant information or examples from the text. Allow students to use their Gists to support their opinions during the discussion.

      Bridging P1.3.3

      Bridging ELLs can benefit from being able to reference both their Gists and the academic vocabulary during the Whole Group Discussion. Encourage Bridging ELLs to build on others’ responses and to offer opinions using complex sentences.
