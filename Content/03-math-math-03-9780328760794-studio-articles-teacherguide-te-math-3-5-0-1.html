LESSON 1: Patterns in Multiplication

Lesson Overview

Students review the facts learned in Unit 3.3 by repeating a multiplication chant. They examine a multiplication table showing only the products studied in Unit 3.3. They look for patterns in these products and use the patterns to find products they have not yet learned.

Key Concepts

Mathematics has a very beautiful, consistent structure. Noticing that structure and using it is integral to learning and understanding multiplication. The patterns in the multiplication table are based on the distributive and the commutative properties. For example, a pattern you might notice in the multiplication table is that the product of 2 and 4 plus the product of 3 and 4 equals the product of 5 and 4.

This pattern holds true everywhere in the multiplication table and can be generalized as ac + bc = (a + b)c, which you may recognize as a statement of the distributive property.

Note that students do not learn the names or formal definitions of mathematical properties until Grade 4.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Generate interest in multiplication patterns

  • Identify and use the patterns in the multiplication table to better understand the commutative and distributive properties

  • Use patterns to predict products of unknown multiplication facts
