Opening

Task 1: Vocabulary

    • Use the following questions and discussion points to discuss with students the meanings of three vocabulary words from Boxes for Katje: threadbare, bitter, sparingly.

    • Read this sentence aloud: “People stopped worrying about the holes in their threadbare coats.”

      • Ask students what they think threadbare means. Accept answers that mean “very thin from too much use.”

    • Explain that the word bitter has more than one meaning. Invite students to say what they think the word means. Explain that one meaning of the word is to have a strong, sharp, and unpleasant taste. Have students name foods that are bitter.

      • Then read this sentence: “Weeks passed, and winter roared in, snow-deep and bitter cold, the worst winter anyone could remember.” Point out that in this sentence bitter means strong, sharp, and unpleasantly cold.

    • Read this sentence: “They huddled close to their small fires, ate sparingly from their almost empty cupboards, shivered, and prayed.”

      • Ask students: Do you think the people of Olst ate a lot at this time? Why or why not? Confirm that to eat sparingly is to eat a little so that it will last.
