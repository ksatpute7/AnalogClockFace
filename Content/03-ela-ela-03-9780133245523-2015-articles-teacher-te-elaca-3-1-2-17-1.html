Opening

Task 1: Spelling Strategies Review

    • Preface this task with a short discussion on why spelling skills are still needed even in this age of technology. Offer these reasons about why spelling is still an important skill:

      • It helps you with your reading.
      • Even with programs like “SpellCheck” and “AutoCorrect,” the writer needs to proofread and recognize errors.
      • Incorrect spelling interferes with meaning.
      • People (among them future employers) judge people by their spelling (and their vocabulary).

    • Now proceed with this discussion about spelling strategies.

    • Ask students to think about what they do when they do not know how to spell a word when they are writing and they do not have access to technology. Ask them to Turn and Talk with a partner and discuss spelling strategies that have worked in the past. Encourage them to use the following sentence frame:

      When I didn’t know how to spell a word, I _____.

    • Remind students that a strategy means a plan of action, or what they will do to solve a spelling problem. Tell them that the phonics they are learning will also help them with their spelling.

    • Title a chart “Spelling Strategies.”

    • Ask students to share their strategies with the class. Explain and add any strategies students do not address. Your list may look like the following:

      Spelling Strategies

      • Look for the word on the Word Wall.
      • Ask a friend for help.
      • Try to sound it out.
      • Write any smaller words you hear in the word.
      • Listen for prefixes or suffixes.
      • Clap the syllables of the word and spell the word one syllable at a time.
      • Close your eyes and picture what the word looks like when you see it in a book.
      • Look the word up in the dictionary.
      • Circle the words that you know are not spelled correctly in writing.

    • When the list is complete, display it for students to reference.
