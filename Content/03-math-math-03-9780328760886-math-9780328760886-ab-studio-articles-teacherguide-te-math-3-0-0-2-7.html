Task 7: Prepare a Presentation

Lesson Guide

Have students choose what they think is the most difficult riddle and prepare a presentation about their strategies for solving it. Remind students to think about their list of how to make a clear presentation.

Preparing for Ways of Thinking

As you observe partners working, listen carefully to their dialog and look for the following.

  • Is the listener asking questions when the speaker is unclear?

  • Do both partners explain their processes, even if they each did something similar?

  • Do students understand the solutions to each riddles?

Identify several student pairs to present during Ways of Thinking. Choose students who both correctly and incorrectly matched the cards—especially students who show the common issues identified in Interventions. Look for partners who initially disagree on a solution and critique each other’s reasoning clearly.

Mathematical Practices

Mathematical Practice 3: Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others.

  • When students critique a solution to a riddle or argue for or against another student's solution, they are using this math practice.

Mathematical Practice 4: Model with mathematics.

  • Students show their work in presentations using drawings or other visuals.
