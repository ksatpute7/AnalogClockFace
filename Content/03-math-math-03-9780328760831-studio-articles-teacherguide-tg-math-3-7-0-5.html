LESSON 5: Number of Equal-Length Pieces

Lesson Overview

Students chant along with the multiplication animation for the eights. They solve division math stories that involve finding the number of equal-length pieces. They prepare a presentation that includes a model of the problem, a division equation, a multiplication equation, and an answer to the problem.

Key Concepts

In grouping situations, the size of the piece distributed to each group is known, and you compute to find the number of groups.

This is a typical equal-groups math story that involves quantities that are measured:

  • Ms. Perkins has 12 feet of ribbon. She uses 4 feet of ribbon to wrap a present. How many presents can she wrap?

Acting out this problem might involve an action such as measuring out a length of four feet, cutting it off, then measuring out another length of four feet, and so on, until there is not enough ribbon left to make another four-foot length.

The total and the length of each piece are known. The problem asks how many pieces can be made. That question can be answered by asking what number times 4 makes 12, or ☐×4=12 ?

  • ELL:

    When introducing new words or using words that students recently learned (whose meaning might not be fresh in their minds), repeat the new/newer words at a slower pace, write them down if possible, and ask students to repeat after you. Be sure ELLs feel comfortable with the pronunciation. Additionally, allow ELLs to use a dictionary.

  • SWD:

    Students with tactile learning styles benefit from acting out the context of the problem with concrete models like measuring ribbon. To assist in transitioning learners from concrete to visual and representational, have them work with the concrete model and then sketch a visual representation of the situation. If they are able, guide them to the abstract, too, by having them write the division equation that represents the problem.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Model finding the number of equal-length pieces.

  • Reinforce the meaning of the dividend, divisor, and quotient.

  • Write division and multiplication equations to represent a math story.

  • Compare and contrast grouping division with sharing division.
