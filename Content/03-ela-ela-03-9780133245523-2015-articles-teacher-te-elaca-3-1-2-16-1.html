Opening

Task 1: Vocabulary

    • Tell students that they will focus on three new vocabulary words in this task: incinerate, located, and extended.

    • Use the following questions and points to discuss these words with students:

      • Begin by reading this sentence from What is a Community? from A to Z: “Town workers remove garbage from streets and incinerate it, bury it in landfill sites, or recycle it.”
      • Tell students: The workers do three things with garbage. What are they? (incinerate, bury, recycle)

        Based on this information, what do you think incinerate might mean? Allow volunteers to share their ideas, and then explain that incinerate means to burn completely.
      • Ask: Where is our school located? Explain that located means to be in a particular place. Use this sentence from the Common Read to further illustrate the meaning of this word: “Some communities are located on islands or high up on mountains.” Ask students: Where is your favorite park located?
      • Ask: What does an extension cord help you to do? (It makes the electrical cord longer.) Then read aloud the following sentence from What is a Community? from A to Z: “There are many kinds of families—two-parent families, single-parent families, foster families, and extended families.”

        Say to students: Using what you know about the word extension, what do you think an extended family is? Elicit from the students that an extended family would be one made larger by additional family members, such as grandparents or cousins.

    • Remind students to enter the three words into their Personal Notebook and to write a short definition for each.
