LESSON 6: Groups of Two

Lesson Overview

Students repeat the twos chant during the class Opening. Find a few minutes during the day to recite the fives chant as well. Next, students learn how to use a Tape Diagram to model multiplication situations. They work with multiplication in the context of distance traveled. They consider what happens to the total distance traveled if 1 more 2-foot hop is hopped. Finally, students look for structure in the Twos Multiplication Table.

Key Concepts

Multiplication can be used for more than finding the total number of objects in equal-sized groups or the total length of equal-sized objects placed end to end. The distance traveled is used in this lesson to continue developing students’ understanding that the multiplicand is a unit that can be counted. Each distance of 2 feet is counted. Each 2-foot distance is a group.

Students should notice that when the number of groups increases by 1, the product increases by 2. Later in third grade, students will be expected to make a generalization: when the number of groups goes up by 1, the product goes up by the number in each group.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Learn the twos chant to help memorize the twos facts

  • Use a Tape Diagram to model and solve a problem involving hops of 2 feet

  • Understand that when the number of hops goes up by 1, the total distance goes up by the size of the hop, 2 feet

  • Examine the patterns in the Twos Multiplication Table

Materials

  • Manipulatives, such as snap cubes or counters
