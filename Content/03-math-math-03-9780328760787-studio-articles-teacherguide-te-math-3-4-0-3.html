LESSON 3: Rectangles and Squares

Lesson Overview

Students will construct and define squares. They will explore how squares are related to rectangles and build rectangles to meet specific conditions.

Content Standards

3.G—Geometry

Reason with shapes and their attributes.

  • 1. Understand that shapes in different categories (e.g., rhombuses, rectangles, and others) may share attributes (e.g., having four sides), and that the shared attributes can define a larger category (e.g., quadrilaterals). Recognize rhombuses, rectangles, and squares as examples of quadrilaterals, and draw examples of quadrilaterals that do not belong to any of these subcategories.

Key Concepts

A rectangle is a quadrilateral with four right angles. A square also has four right angles. Therefore, a square is a rectangle. Opposite sides of a rectangle are the same length—this is also true for a square. In addition, adjacent sides of a square  are also the same length. So, a square is a rectangle with one additional attribute. We refer to rectangles that have adjacent sides of different lengths as nonsquare rectangles.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Understand that a square is a rectangle.

  • Understand that a rectangle can be a square.

Materials

  • Rectangular (nonsquare) piece of paper (1 for each student)

  • Scissors (1 for each student)

  • Set square (1 for each student)
