Task 3: Word Choice

    • Explain the importance of word choice. Model how to read closely and think about why the author uses particular words.

    • Reread the first three pages of the text to the students. Focus on the sentence: “‘The land of plenty,’ she whispered.”

    • Think Aloud:

      When I read this text, I noticed that the author used the word whispered to describe how Katje said the words. Whispered means she said the word softly. The author could have used said Katje, or exclaimed Katje, but she used whispered. I think she used the word whispered to show that Katje was surprised and amazed by this box. This makes the moment important and the author wants us to understand just how precious this box is.

    • Work through a second example with the students.

      • Have them read the sentence: “For several moments the three savored the almost forgotten taste.” Ask students what the word savored means. (Take time to enjoy something)
      • What other words could the author have used in place of savored? (tasted, ate)
      • Why did the author use the word savored?

    • Have students find and highlight the words precious, hollered, stumbling and miracle, and think about what they mean and why the author used these specific words.

      Emerging P1.3.8

      Have Emerging ELLs distinguish how different words produce different effects on the audience. Pair Emerging ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency. Point out the words precious, hollered, stumbling, and miracle. The partner can explain what the specific words mean.

      Expanding P1.3.8

      Have Expanding ELLs distinguish how different words with similar meanings produce shades of meaning and different effects on the audience. Pair Expanding ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency. Point out the words precious, hollered, stumbling, and miracle. Using a bilingual dictionary, students can search for the equivalent words in their native language. Provide a sentence frame such as:

      • Is there a word for _____ in your native language?

      Bridging P1.3.8

      Have Bridging ELLs distinguish how multiple different words with similar meanings produce shades of meaning and different effects on the audience. Point out the words precious, hollered, stumbling, and miracle. Allow students to use a bilingual dictionary for this task. Show them how to use the definitions they find to deepen their understanding of the selected words.
