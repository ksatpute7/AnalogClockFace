Task 5: Make Connections

Lesson Guide

Possible support for presentations includes the following.

  • Suggest alternate means of making the presentations, such as making a video or audio recording that is shared with classmates

  • When groups are presenting, consider assigning different roles to group members (materials manager, note-taker, director, etc.) who are less comfortable

  • Provide additional rehearsal opportunities and have students use tools, such as cue cards, to reduce anxiety and reliance on memory

Ask the students you identified during Preparing for Ways of Thinking to present their work.

Encourage students to incorporate into their presentations the mathematics they used as well as information about the tools, resources, or game.

Depending on students’ level of understanding of place value, spend time discussing how place value relates to addition and subtraction. Where appropriate, guide students to think about how place value can help them make sense of real-world problems.

Finish the discussion by returning to the Class Norms list and other lists the class has created over the week. Ask students if they have any more suggestions they would like to add to any of the charts. Write students’ suggestions on the charts and in any shared Notebook pages.

SWD:

While all students should be working on speaking and listening skills, some students with disabilities may be reluctant to speak in front of peers for a variety of reasons. Consider offering these students alternate means of presenting their work.
