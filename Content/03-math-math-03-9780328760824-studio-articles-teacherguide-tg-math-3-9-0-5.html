LESSON 5: More Area of Rectangles

Lesson Overview

Students will calculate the area of rectangles when given their length and width.

Key Concepts

The area of a rectangle can be found by multiplying its length by its width. Imagine a grid of square units. The length indicates how many square units are in one row, since each square is one length unit long. The width indicates the number of rows. Multiplying the length by the width is equivalent to multiplying the number of square units in each row by the number of rows.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Calculate the area of rectangles given the length and the width.
