LESSON 5: Participating in a Gallery

Lesson Overview

Students discuss the ways that classroom habits and routines can help them meet their responsibility as a math student. They learn what a Gallery is and how to choose a Gallery problem to work on. Then they choose one of four Gallery problems that introduce the unitâ€™s technology resources and deal with the place-value concepts students learned in the unit.The Lesson 6 Gallery is really just a part of Lesson 5 in this unit. In future units, Galleries will be separate, multi-day enrichment lessons, and students who are caught up will work on these to give you additional time to review with other students.

Content Standards

Standards will vary with respect to the Gallery problems students select.

Key Concepts

The Gallery problems serve two purposes: they introduce students to different parts of the technology that they will use during the year (The drawing area in the Notebook, and tools such as the Number Line tool), and they give students a chance to practice and apply what they have learned about place value.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Think about how to take responsibility for their learning

  • Learn about the Gallery and how to choose a problem

  • Learn about different aspects of the technology that they will use throughout the year

  • Practice and apply place-value concepts to solve problems

Materials

  • Chart paper and markers to make Responsibilities poster
