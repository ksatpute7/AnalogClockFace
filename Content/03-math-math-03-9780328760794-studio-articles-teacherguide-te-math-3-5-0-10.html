Key Concepts

When multiplying three or more factors, the order in which they are grouped does not matter. Consider the formula for finding the volume of a rectangular prism. This formula is usually given as l x w x h. To carry out the indicated multiplication, the factors must be grouped in one of these ways. (l x w) x h or l x (w x h).

Of course, volume can also be calculated as (l x h) x w. Notice that this requires the use of both the associative and the commutative properties. First apply the commutative property: w x h = h x w. Then use the associative property to group length and height: (l x h) x w.
