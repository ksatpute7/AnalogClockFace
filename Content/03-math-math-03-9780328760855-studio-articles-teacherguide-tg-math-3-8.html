Type of Unit: Concept

Length of Unit: 13 days

Prior Knowledge

Students should be able to:

  • Read and write whole numbers up to three digits.

  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide whole numbers.

  • Understand the base-10 numeration system.

  • Understand equal partitioning of geometric figures.

Lesson Flow

In the first four lessons, students work with unit fractions. Students look at unit fractions represented as area models and on number lines. They express lengths that are 1 equal part of 1 meter, and they divide a whole rectangular piece of clay into equal parts, labeling 1 part as a fraction.

Then students name fractions that represent more than 1 equal part of a whole. They discover that all fractions larger than 1 equal part of the whole are composed of unit fractions. To reinforce their understanding of what a fraction is, they match different representations of the same fraction: area models, number lines, word names, and numbers.

Next, students use a number line to understand fractions greater than or equal to 1.

Then students use number lines and area models to help them understand and identify equivalent fractions. Students compare fractions with the same numerator, thinking about the size of the unit fractions to make their comparison.

Students complete a Self Check and match equivalent fractions in a card sort. The unit ends with a Gallery of fraction problems and a Unit Assessment.

Contents

  1. Lesson 1: A Part of One Meter 7

    Students will express lengths that are 1 equal part of 1 meter.

  2. Lesson 2: Parts of One Piece of Clay 15

    Students will divide a whole rectangular piece of clay into equal parts and will label 1 part as a fraction.

  3. Lesson 3: The Number Line 23

    Students will learn how a number line is like a ruler. They will divide a whole length into equal parts and find what fraction 1 part is of the whole.

  4. Lesson 4: Unit Fractions 31

    Students will learn about unit fractions. They will look at unit fractions represented as area of a figure and on number lines.

  5. Lesson 5: More Than One Equal Part 39

    Students will give measurements that represent more than 1 equal part of a whole.

  6. Lesson 6: Fraction Representations 46

    Students will match different representations of the same fraction.

  7. Lesson 7: Greater Than or Equal to One 53

    Students will use a number line to understand fractions greater than or equal to 1.

  8. Lesson 8: Equivalent Fractions 62

    Students will use number lines and area models to help them understand and identify equivalent fractions.

  9. Lesson 9: Comparing Fractions 73

    Students will compare fractions with the same numerator. Then they will complete a Self Check task.

  10. Lesson 10: Putting It Together 86

    Students will review their work on the Self Check task. Then they will work with a partner to sort tiles to match equivalent fractions.

  11. Lessons 11–12: Gallery 94

    Students who have a clear understanding of the content thus far in the unit will work on Gallery problems of their choosing.

  12. Lesson 13: Unit Assessment 101

    Students will take a Unit Assessment to find out what they have learned about fractions.

Mathematical Practices

  1. Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them.

  2. Reason abstractly and quantitatively.

  3. Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others.

  4. Model with mathematics.

  5. Use appropriate tools strategically.

  6. Attend to precision.

  7. Look for and make use of structure.

  8. Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning.
