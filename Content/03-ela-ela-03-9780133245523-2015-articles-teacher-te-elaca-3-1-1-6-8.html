Task 8: Vocabulary

    • Use the following questions and discussion points to discuss these vocabulary words with students: urged, savored, generosity.

    • Read aloud this sentence from Boxes for Katje: “Katje’s mama came to stand beside her. ‘Open it,’ she urged.”

      • Ask volunteers to say what they think urged means. Explain that it means to persuade or encourage someone, usually with some energy.
      • Tell students: Katje’s mom wanted to see what was inside the package, and Katje was taking too long to open it!

    • Next, read aloud this sentence from the Common Read: “For several moments the three savored the almost forgotten taste.”

      • Have students mime savoring chocolate. If necessary, model the action first.

    • Then read aloud this sentence from the book: “‘Wonder baar!’ shouted Dr. Bos, when he looked in the box. ‘Such generosity!’”

      • Ask students if the word generosity sounds like another word they may know. (generous)
      • Have students say something they could do that would show generosity. (If necessary, explain that Wonder baar is his way of saying wonderful.)
