LESSON 4: Multiplying Multiples of 10

Lesson Overview

Students continue the practice of chanting the multiplication chants to attain fluency. Students solve a problem involving 3 groups of 50 and share strategies based on place value and properties for multiplying by 10. Next, students apply their learning by solving similar problems.

Content Standards

3.NBT—Number and Operations in Base Ten

Use place value understanding and properties of operations to perform multi-digit arithmetic.

3. Multiply one-digit whole numbers by multiples of 10 in the range 10–90 (e.g., 9 × 80, 5 × 60) using strategies based on place value and the properties of operations.

Key Concepts

Students extend their understanding of multiplying tens and of the associative property to a new situation: multiplying multiples of 10 by single-digit numbers. The image of three crates of apples is intentionally arranged with apples in 5 rows of 10 to help students recall and make use of prior learning. In Grade 2, students learned that 50 equals 5 tens. In Grade 3, students learned to multiply 10 by a single-digit number, and then students learned that when there are three factors in an expression, they can start by multiplying any two factors. This lesson brings these ideas together.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Understand and use strategies based on place value and properties to multiply multiples of 10
