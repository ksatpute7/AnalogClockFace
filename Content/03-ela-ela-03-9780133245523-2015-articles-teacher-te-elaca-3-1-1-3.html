Lesson 3

Lesson Overview

This lesson introduces the names and defines the characteristics of the genres students will encounter in their reading. Understanding the characteristics of genres supports students’ comprehension. An awareness of genres also encourages wide reading, which has benefits in terms of vocabulary development and concept development.

Materials

  • Chart paper and markers
  • Independent Reading chart
  • Materials to provide sentence frames (for ELLs)

Lesson Preparation

  • Read the lesson and student content.
  • Anticipate student difficulties and identify the differentiation options you will choose for working with your students.
  • Have examples of books from different genres (realistic fiction, fantasy, informational text, poetry, drama, fairytale, and memoir) to show the class.
  • Make sure the Independent Reading chart is accessible for you to add more entries.
  • Decide how you want students to keep a Reading Log: as a Notebook page within the app, using a free app they download, online at sites like Reading-Rewards or BiblioNasium, or in a paper notebook. They should track the title and author of the book, the date, the number of pages they read, any questions or comments they have about the book, and a rating (which they can enter each time or only after they’ve finished their book). Do some research to determine which method will work best for you and your students.
  • Prepare sentence frames for ELLs to use in Task 1.
