UNIT 12

Find the Unknown Number

Type of Unit: Problem Solving

Length of Unit: 14 days

You will solve math stories to explore the relationships between multiplication and division and between addition and subtraction. You will then use these relationships to estimate, solve, and check the answers to math stories involving all four operations. You can write a single equation to represent a two-step word problem. Sometimes you will use parentheses to show which operation to do first. Other times you can use the order of operations. In some equations, you will use a letter, or variable, to represent the unknown numbers that you are trying to find.

Prior Knowledge

Students should be able to:

  • Use addition and subtraction within 100 to solve two-step word problems.

  • Solve word problems involving money.

  • Use multiplication and division within 100 to solve word problems in situations involving equal groups, arrays, and measurement quantities.

  • Multiply and divide within 100.

  • Know from memory all products of two one-digit numbers.

Lesson Flow

In this unit, students gain experience in solving two-step problems by using equations with the four operations—addition, multiplication, subtraction, and division. Although students have solved a variety of one-step and two-step problems, this unit focuses on allowing students to solve problems and learn how to represent two-step problems with one equation. During the process of solving a variety of two-step problems, students will be introduced to the order of operations and the use of parentheses in simple cases. Students will also learn how to check the reasonableness of an answer using the relationships between operations, number sense, and estimates.

Contents

  1. Lesson 1: Multiplication and Division

    Students will review the relationship between multiplication and division by solving a problem involving the weight of meatballs.

  2. Lesson 2: Addition and Subtraction

    Students will review the relationship between addition and subtraction by solving a problem about orange and yellow tulips sold at a flower shop.

  3. Lesson 3: Which Operation?

    Students will solve word problems and check the answers using the relationship among operations.

  4. Lesson 4: How Much Change?

    Students will see how using parentheses can help represent a two-step problem with only one equation.

  5. Lesson 5: Solving Two-step Problems

    Students will make a diagram and write a single equation to solve a two-step problem involving the cost of goody bags for a birthday party.

  6. Lesson 6: Baseball Cards

    Students will make a diagram and write an equation to help solve a two-step problem involving sharing baseball cards.

  7. Lesson 7: How Many Girls?

    Students will make a diagram and write an equation to help solve a two-step problem involving the number of girls at a park.

  8. Lesson 8: Cans Of Cat Food

    Students will make a diagram and write an equation to help solve a two-step problem involving cans of cat food.

  9. Lesson 9: How Much Money?

    Students will make a diagram and write an equation to help solve a two-step problem involving the money Jasper is saving to buy a microscope.

  10. Lesson 10: How Many Pies?

    Students will make a diagram and write an equation to help solve a two-step problem involving the number of pies José and his father baked.

  11. Lesson 11: Solving Two-Step Problems

    Students will choose a problem to solve and create a presentation showing their solution.

  12. Lessons 12–13: Gallery

    Students who have a clear understanding of the content thus far in the unit will work on Gallery problems of their choosing.

  13. Lesson 14: Unit Assessment

    The final assessment is computer-scored items and performance tasks that test students on writing equations and solving two-step problems with unknown values.

Common Core State Standards for Mathematics

This unit focuses on the following Common Core State Standards for Mathematics:

3.OA Operations and Algebraic Thinking

Solve problems involving the four operations, and identify and explain patterns in arithmetic.

8. Solve two-step word problems using the four operations. Represent these problems using equations with a letter standing for the unknown quantity. Assess the reasonableness of answers using mental computation and estimation strategies including rounding.3

3 This standard is limited to problems posed with whole numbers and having whole-number answers; students should know how to perform operations in the conventional order when there are no parentheses to specify a particular order (Order of Operations).

Mathematical Practices

  1. Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them.

  2. Reason abstractly and quantitatively.

  3. Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others.

  4. Model with mathematics.

  5. Use appropriate tools strategically.

  6. Attend to precision.

  7. Look for and make use of structure.

  8. Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning.
