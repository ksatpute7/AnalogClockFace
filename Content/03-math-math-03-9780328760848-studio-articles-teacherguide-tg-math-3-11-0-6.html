LESSON 6: Solving Weight Problems

Lesson Overview

Students use addition and subtraction to solve word problems involving weight.

Content Standards

  • 2. Measure and estimate liquid volumes and masses of objects using standard units of grams (g), kilograms (kg), and liters (l). Add, subtract, multiply, or divide to solve one-step word problems involving masses or volumes that are given in the same units, e.g., by using drawings (such as a beaker with a measurement scale) to represent the problem.

Key Concepts

Measuring weight involves assigning a unique numeric value to a particular attribute of a physical object. Mathematical operations can be used to solve problems based on these values. Even in rudimentary one-step, one-operation problem solving, students can see the power of mathematics as a tool to describe and manipulate the physical world.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Solve word problems involving measuring weight in grams and kilograms using addition and subtraction.

Materials

  • Pan balance and weights for Challenge Problem (at least one for every four students)

Preparation

  • Set up pan balances and weights in a designated area so students can come up and weigh objects for the Challenge Problem.
