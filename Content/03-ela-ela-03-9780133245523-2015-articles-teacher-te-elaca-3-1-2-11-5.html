Task 5: Writing a Gist

    • Explain that a Gist is a summary in 20 words or fewer. For both fiction and nonfiction, the Gist summarizes the most important ideas in the text.

      Check for understanding of the term “Gist” by having students explain it back to you in their own words.

    • Now you will teach how to write a Gist. Have students turn to the B page in What is a Community? from A to Z. Ask them to reread this page silently.

    • Next, display 20 blanks and work with the students to fill in the 20 blanks (or fewer) as they summarize the text with you. The blanks should be filled in with their own words, but based on the reading. You and the class will negotiate as you fill in the blanks: which words are most important? Which can we leave out?

    • Here is a sample Gist for the B page:

      • All communities have buildings. The buildings come in all sizes and shapes. Buildings can be schools, churches, skyscrapers, and homes. (20 words)

    • If time permits, follow the same procedure for the C page. This time have students work in pairs or in small groups.

    • Gist writing is a skill that will develop over time. Be sure to address this skill in other content areas such as science and social studies.

      Emerging P1.3.2

      Emerging ELLs can benefit from working in a small group. Provide support as they complete the procedure for letter C. When paraphrasing texts, encourage ELLs to use key words and short, simple sentences. Allow students to state their Gist orally, counting the total number of words in their home language before writing it down. (It can’t be more than 20.)

      Expanding P1.3.2

      Pair Expanding ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency. As they paraphrase the text to get the Gist, encourage ELLs to write in complete sentences using key words. Invite students to state their Gist orally, counting the total number of words in their home language before writing it down. (It can’t be more than 20.)

      Bridging P1.3.2

      Pair Bridging ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency. As they paraphrase the text to get the Gist, encourage ELLs to write complex sentences with key words from the text.

    • Please note that the number of words is not crucial, but it is suggested here as a maximum limit. Students often want to use more words than necessary when writing a Gist. The goal is to try to represent the page as fully as possible in very few words.
