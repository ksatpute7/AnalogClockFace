LESSONS 7–9: Gallery

Gallery Overview

Allow students who have a clear understanding of the content thus far in the unit to work on Gallery problems of their choosing. You can then use this time to provide additional help to students who need review of the unit's concepts or to assist students who may have fallen behind on work.

Gallery Descriptions

Multiplication Table 58

Students will act as detectives and use the clues in a mixed-up multiplication table to fill in all missing multipliers and multiplicands.

Multiplication Stories 58

For each expression, students will write a math story. They will then solve their math stories.

Cookie Sale 58

Students will put math to work to help Abdul figure out how much money he can earn by selling some cookies.

How Many More? 60

Students will use their knowledge that there are many ways to solve problems. This problem can be solved with many steps or with a few steps. Students will use what they know about the structure of multiplication to discover the way to solve it that uses the fewest steps.

Jasper and Kentasha 60

Students will use what they know about the structure of multiplication to show at least two different ways to solve this problem.

Baseball Cards 60

Students will use what they know about place value and about multiplying by 10 and 100 to help José figure out how many baseball cards he has.

Tacking On Zero 60

Luz uses a trick to find the answer to 7 × 60, but tricks can be tricky. If students don't understand why they work, tricks can lead to mistakes. Students will look at Luz's trick and explain to her why it works.

Dot Array 61

Students will look at an array of dots and show two different ways to calculate the total number of dots.

Science Museum 61

Students will calculate the cost of tickets for students and adults visiting a science museum.

School Assembly 61

Students will be presented with a problem in which two boys are setting up chairs for an assembly. Students will help them figure out how many chairs they set up.

Book Graph 62

In this problem, three teachers made a pictograph showing how many books their students read this summer. Students will use the pictograph to find how many books their students read.

Multiplication Stories

Possible Answers

Answers will vary. Each story problem should do these three things:

  • State the number of groups (as given in the expression).

  • State the number in each group (as given in the expression).

  • Include a question.

Here is one possible math story and one possible solution for each expression.

Cookie Sale

Answers

  1. Possible solution method and answer.

    75 - 30 = 45 Abdul can sell 45 cookies.
    5 × ? = 45 → 5 × 9 = 45 Abdul can sell 9 packages of cookies.
    9 × 4 = 36 Abdul will earn $36 if he sells all 9 packages.

How Many More?

Answers

  1. Kai has 3 × 10 apples

    Zoe has 5 × 10 apples

    5 groups of 10 is 2 more groups of 10 than 3 groups of 10, so Zoe has 2 groups of 10 or 20 more apples than Kai.

Jasper and Kentasha

Answers

  1. Kentasha has 30 more donuts than Jasper.

  2. Ways to find the answer will vary. Here are two possible methods.


    They both have the same number of boxes, but Kentasha has 9 – 6 = 3 more donuts in each box, so Kentasha has 10 groups of 3 or 30 more donuts than Jasper.


    Kentasha has 90 donuts and Jasper has 60 donuts, so 90-60=30. Kentasha has 30 donuts more.

Baseball Cards

Answers

  1. Possible solution steps and answer.

    3 × 100 = 300 There are 300 cards in the boxes he sold.
    9 × 10 = 90 There are 90 cards in the bags he sold.
    65 × 1 = 65 He sold 65 individual cards.
    300 + 90 + 65 = 455 He sold 455 baseball cards in all.

Science Museum

Answers

  1. Possible solution steps and answer.

    30 × 5 = 150 Tickets for 30 children will cost $150.
    ? × 5 = 30 → 6 × 5 = 30 Because there are 6 groups of 5 in 30, 6 adults get in free.
    12 − 6 = 6 6 adults get in free and 6 adults need to buy tickets.
    6 × 10 = 60 Tickets for 6 adults cost $60.
    $150 + $60 = $210 Tickets for all the adults and children will cost $210.

School Assembly

Answers

  1. 30 is 3 tens so 9 × 30 = 9 × 3 × 10

    9 × 3 = 27 and 27 × 10 = 270

    They set up 270 chairs.

Book Graph

Answers

  1. Ms. Perkins's class read 90 books.

    Each book icon stands of 10 books and there are 9 icons.

    9 × 10 = 90

  2. There are two more icons for Mr. Morales's class than there are for Ms. Anderson's class. Mr. Morales's class read 2 tens, or 20 more books, than Ms. Anderson's class.

    2 × 10 = 20

  3. There are 21 book icons for all the classes together.

    21 × 10 = 210

    They read 210 books.

    OR

    Ms. Perkins's class read 90 books.

    Ms. Anderson's class read 5 × 10 = 50 books.

    Mr. Morales's class read 7 × 10 = 70 books.

    90 + 50 + 70 = 210 books

    In all, they read 210 books.
