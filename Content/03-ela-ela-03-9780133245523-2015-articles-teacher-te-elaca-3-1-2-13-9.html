Task 9: Interview Questions

    • Circulate through the room to help students with their questions.

    • Use the guidance in the Interview Preparation document as a scaffold for writers who need additional support.

      Conference with partner groups to model coming up with good questions if SWDs struggle to generate questions for their interview subject.

      Emerging P1.3.2

      Before beginning to write their questions, have Emerging ELLs refer back to the academic vocabulary in Lesson 11. This will activate students’ prior knowledge of what a community is. It would also be helpful to allow students to observe the people they are going to interview prior to writing the questions in order to create a context. Provide students with the wh- words so they can write basic questions.

      Expanding P1.3.2

      Before beginning to write their questions, have Expanding ELLs refer back to the academic vocabulary in Lesson 11. Have students refer to their vocabulary lists as they write the questions. Encourage and prompt students to include this academic vocabulary in their detailed questions.

      Bridging P1.3.2

      Bridging ELLs should have their academic vocabulary lists as a reference when writing the questions with their partners. Prompt students to write complex and compound questions that include the academic vocabulary.
