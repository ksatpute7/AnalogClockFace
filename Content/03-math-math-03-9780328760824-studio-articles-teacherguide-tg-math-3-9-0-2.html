LESSON 2: Which Has the Larger Area?

Lesson Overview

Students will compare the area of rectangles using unit squares. Students will discuss the advantage of using squares as a unit versus other arbitrary units.

Content Standards

3.MD—Measurement and Data

Geometric measurement: understand concepts of area and relate area to multiplication and addition.

  • 5. Recognize area as an attribute of plane figures and understand concepts of area measurement.

    1. A square with side length 1 unit, called “a unit square,” is said to have “one square unit” of area, and can be used to measure area.

    2. A plane figure that can be covered without gaps or overlaps by n unit squares is said to have an area of n square units.

  • 6. Measure areas by counting unit squares (square cm, square m, square in, square ft, and improvised units).

Key Concepts

Area is measured in “square” units. The area of a two-dimensional figure is the number of squares required to cover it completely without gaps or overlaps.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Compare the area of rectangles.

  • Find how much larger one rectangle is than another.

  • ELL: Identifying key words is crucial for students. Spend some time discussing the key vocabulary in this unit.
