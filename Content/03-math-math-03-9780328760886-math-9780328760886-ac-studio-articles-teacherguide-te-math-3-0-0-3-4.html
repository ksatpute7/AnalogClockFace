Task 4: Write Missing Numbers

Lesson Guide

Students may open the Number Chart form and use the drawing layer to write in the missing numbers. They may also take a snapshot of the chart and place it in their Notebooks, and type the missing numbers in the squares using the text tool, or simply list the missing numbers in their Notebooks. See what works best for your class, or for each student, then instruct students accordingly.

Have students work on filling in the missing numbers from the number chart on their own, and writing explanations in their Notebooks, for 2 or 3 minutes. Then have them work with their partner.

Mathematical Practices

Mathematical Practice 1: Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them

  • Students begin their work by first making sense of this new problem by using “ask myself” questions. Then they persevere by using the ideas they generated for what to do when you get stuck.

Mathematical Practice 7: Look for and make use of structure

  • Students identify patterns in numbers chart and use the patterns to write missing numbers.

Interventions

Student does not use “ask myself” questions before solving.

  • What questions could you ask yourself before you start solving this problem in order to help you make sense of the problem?

Student stops working when he or she gets stuck.

  • What can you do if you get stuck on a problem?

  • Look at the chart we made earlier in the lesson.

Student writes numbers that do not fit the pattern.

  • Why did you decide to write that number?

  • What pattern do you see in the numbers chart? How can you use the pattern to write the rest of the numbers?

Student is confused about when to add or subtract 1 to the tens digit or ones digit.

  • What happens as you move left along a row?

  • What happens as you move right along a row?

  • What happens as you move down a column?

  • What happens as you move up a column?

Student does not write the 0 in 160 and 200.

  • What number do I get if I subtract 10 from 213?

  • Use manipulatives or a drawing to help you work on this problem.

Possible Answers

  • Missing numbers are 140, 154, 155, 161, 178, 188, 198, 199, and 200.

  • Students might use these patterns to explain their work:

    • Add 1 to a number on the right of an empty space in the chart.

    • Subtract 1 to a number on the left of an empty space in the chart.

    • Add 10 to a number above an empty space in the chart.

    • Subtract 10 from a number below an empty space in the chart.

  • Circulate the room to encourage and confirm conversations with partners comparing and explaining work to one another.
