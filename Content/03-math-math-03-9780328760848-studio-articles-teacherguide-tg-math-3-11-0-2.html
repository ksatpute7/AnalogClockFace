LESSON 2: How Much Does it Hold?

Lesson Overview

Students measure the volume of containers using a liter cube.

Content Standards

3.MD—Measurement and Data

Solve problems involving measurement and estimation of intervals of time, liquid volumes, and masses of objects.

  • 2. Measure and estimate liquid volumes and masses of objects using standard units of grams (g), kilograms (kg), and liters (l). Add, subtract, multiply, or divide to solve one-step word problems involving masses or volumes that are given in the same units, e.g., by using drawings (such as a beaker with a measurement scale) to represent the problem.

Key Concepts

The scale on a measuring device such as a liter cube represents a type of number line. When students determine the volume of the liter cube, they are assigning a numeric value to a property of a physical object.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Make precise measurements of volume in milliliters and liters.

  • Develop an internalized sense of milliliters and liters through hands-on experience with measuring volume.

Materials

For each group of four students:

  • Liter cube

  • Small container with a volume of between 300 and 800 mL

  • Large container, such as a large bowl or a bucket, with a volume of 3–5 L

  • Rice (or water) to fill the containers (enough for each group to fill the large container)
