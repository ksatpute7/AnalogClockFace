LESSON 1: Patterns in Addition

Lesson Overview

Students will find patterns in the addition table. Then, they will use a pattern to help them understand a strategy for addition.

Key Concepts

The strategy of compensation involves adding to or subtracting from one addend to change it to a multiple of 10 (or some other number that is easy to add) and then compensating by performing the opposite operation on the other addend. The sum of the new addends will be the same as the sum of the original addends.

For example, consider the problem 37 + 26. We can add 3 to 37 to get 40, and then compensate by subtracting 3 from 26.

The new problem, 40 + 23, is easy to solve mentally. And, it has the same sum as 37 + 26 because, although we added 3, we also subtracted 3, essentially adding 0.

37+26=40+23=63

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Identify patterns in the addition table

  • Understand and apply the compensation strategy for addition
