Task 4: Boxes for Katje

    • Set the context for the reading using the notes on the annotation.

    • Consider showing students where Olst, Netherlands, is on a map.

      Emerging P1.3.5

      Have Emerging ELLs demonstrate active listening to Boxes for Katje by completing basic sentence frames with prompting and substantial support. For example:

      • The setting of the story is _____.
      • The characters are _____.
      • The problem is _____.

      Expanding P1.3.5

      Have Expanding ELLs demonstrate active listening to Boxes for Katje by completing detailed sentence frames with occasional prompting and moderate support. For example:

      • The setting is _____.
      • Living in Olst, Holland, is probably easy/hard because _____.
      • The boxes from Rosie help by _____.

      Bridging P1.3.5

      Have Bridging ELLs demonstrate active listening to Boxes for Katje by completing detailed sentence frames with minimal prompting and light support. Students at this level shouldn’t need simple language sentence frames but ones that are more complex. For example:

      • I know the setting is _____ because _____.
      • The evidence that shows me that the boxes from Rosie help/do not help the community is _____.

    • Begin to read Boxes for Katje with students, stopping at the points indicated in the annotated text. As you read, define the vocabulary words: generosity, savored, threadbare, urged, sparingly, bitter. Keep in mind that one of the purposes for reading this is to enjoy and understand the story. The first read of the Common Read will help students to get at the text base—what is the text saying explicitly?

    • Check for student understanding by asking the three questions that appear in this task.
