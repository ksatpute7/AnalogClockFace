Task 4: Revision Strategies

    • Review the meaning of revision. Point out the word vision and briefly explain the prefix re-. Explain that when we revise something, we look at it again. Explain that all writers go through the revision process.

    • Title a chart “Revision Strategies.” Ask students to think about the things they have done in the past to improve their writing. Make a list of their ideas. You may need to remind them of some of the things that they have done so far to revise their writing. The list may include the following:

      Revision Strategies

      • Use the rubric to assess the writing.
      • Read your piece aloud. Try to clear up confusing parts.
      • Look for fragments.
      • Think about what the reader will need to know.
      • Add or delete information.
      • Move things around.
      • Give feedback to and receive feedback from others (Response Groups, Writer Conferences, Partner Reading, Author’s Chair).
      • Think carefully about word choice (strong verbs, descriptive adjectives, and adverbs).
      • Check for run-on sentences.
      • Consider using conjunctions to make the meaning of some sentences clearer.
      • Look at the work of mentor authors.

      If students need additional support, give them a revising and editing checklist to work through while making revisions. Instruct them to go through one sentence at a time from the beginning, correcting one item (e.g., punctuation) at a time.

      Emerging P2.3.5

      Have Emerging ELLs focus their revision work on word choice and modifying their sentences to add details. Bring the group together, and model how to expand sentences with strong verbs, descriptive adjectives, and adverbs, using one student’s interview report. Have the group jointly revise a sentence. Send students off to work in small groups with the goal of revising three more sentences.

      Expanding P2.3.5

      Have Expanding ELLs focus their revision strategies on word choice and modifying their sentences to add details. Provide a model of how to expand sentences with strong verbs, descriptive adjectives, and adverbs. Have students work in partnerships to revise their interview reports.

      Bridging P2.3.5

      Have Bridging ELLs focus their revision strategies on word choice and modifying their sentences to add details. During your conference with students, suggest ways to expand simple sentences into compound-complex sentences. Allow students to work in partnerships during the revision process, if they desire; otherwise, they can work independently.

    • Reiterate that revision involves refining and reworking the piece, and considering whether the piece effectively meets its purpose, addresses its audience, reflects the writer’s intention, and meets the expectations defined by the rubric.
