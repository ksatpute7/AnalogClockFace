Task 1: Strengthen Mathematical Character

Lesson Guide

Explain to students that during the past four lessons they used different rituals and routines, such as critiquing classmates’ reasoning, using “ask myself” questions, and applying qualities of effective presentations. Practicing these rituals and routines strengthens students’ mathematical character by providing them with strategies for approaching mathematical situations and for working together to build a mathematical community.

Ask students the following question.

  • What else can you do to be active in class and take responsibility for your own learning?

Have students think about the question individually and then turn and talk to their partner.

If students are having trouble thinking of ideas, suggest that they think about the different parts of class time and the different ways they work during those times: working individually, working with partners, the Ways of Thinking discussion, and so on. Ask students the following questions.

  • What kinds of things do you already do during these parts of a lesson?

  • Can you use those ideas to think of other things you could do?

These hints are given to students as ideas for learning with your classmates.

  • Tell your ideas to your classmates.

  • Talk about the questions that your classmates have about your work.

  • Ask questions.

  • Take notes about different ways of thinking about a problem.

These Hints are given to students as ideas for learning on your own.

  • Check if your answer is reasonable.

  • Use diagrams and complete sentences.

  • Ask yourself questions about your work to improve it.

  • Solve the same problem using different methods.

  • Solve the same problem again using different numbers and look for patterns.

Ask students to share their ideas as a class. Record students’ ideas on a Responsibilities chart that you will keep posted throughout the year. Review each suggestion for clarity. Take a photo and share it with the students.

SWD:

It is important that students learn the routines early on and that the teacher adheres to them strictly. The simplicity and predictability of the routines help students feel more proactive and confident in the classroom.
