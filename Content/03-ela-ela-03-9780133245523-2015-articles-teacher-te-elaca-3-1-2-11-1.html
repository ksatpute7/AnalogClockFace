Opening

Task 1: Vocabulary

    • The six vocabulary words you will focus on for the next few lessons are taken from the Common Read, What is a Community? from A to Z: community, resemble, provide, ensure, constantly, situated. Incorporate them into your teaching as much as possible.

    • Use the following questions and discussion points to introduce students to four of the words: community, provide, constantly, and situated.

      Cultural Connection

      Have ELLs name a few things in their culture that create a community. (They can include celebrations, traditions, food, holidays, beliefs, and so on.) Model an example from your culture:

      • One of the special things in my culture that brings us together is we celebrate _____.

    • Ask students:

      • Which is a community: people who belong to the same book club or a person who writes a book? (people who belong to the same book club)

    • Restate that a community is a group of people who live in the same place or have a particular characteristic in common.

      • Have students name a community they belong to.

    • Explain that to provide something means to supply something that is needed. Say that parents and caregivers provide students with food and shelter. Ask students to name other examples of people and organizations that provide goods or services.

    • Tell students:

      • For example, the Red Cross provides aid when communities are hit with disasters like floods and earthquakes. Doctors provide medical advice to help people stay healthy.
      • Discuss the meaning of the word provide in the context of this sentence from the Common Read: “Some apartment buildings provide homes for hundreds of people.”

    • Say to students:

      • My friend talks constantly about a trip she is taking soon. Do you think she is excited about it? (Yes!)
      • Explain that the word constantly means continuously, over a period of time.
      • Discuss the meaning of the word constantly in the context of this sentence is from the Common Read: “As communities develop, they are constantly changing.”

    • Tell students:

      • Our school is situated in (name the neighborhood or part of town where your school is located). Explain that when something is situated in a place, it is built or put there.

    • Ask the class:

      • Where is the basement of a house situated? (below the ground floor)

    • Have students write these words in their Personal Notebook and provide a short definition for each.

      Emerging P1.3.12

      Emerging ELLs can benefit from being aware of how cognates can help them understand the meaning of words in English. Cognates are words derived from the same origin or root word. Cognates are often spelled similarly in more than one language and they may also share similar meanings. If you have Spanish-speaking ELLs in your class, point out that the English word community and the Spanish word comunidad share the same meaning and are spelled similarly. Highlight that students can use their native language to help them understand the meaning of some English words. This is true for all of the focus words:

      • provide (English) vs. proveer (Spanish)
      • constantly (English) vs. constantemente (Spanish)
      • situate (English) vs. situar (Spanish)
