Opening

Task 1: Grammar and Language: Function of Verbs

    • Ask students if they know what a verb is. They may respond that a verb is an action word. This is only partially true and can be misleading since words like is and thinks are verbs but don’t seem like actions.

    • Redefine verbs for students as “doing words.” Tell them that there are three categories of verbs:

      • physical actions (run and go)
      • mental actions (think and hope)
      • state of being (is and was)

    • Read the following sentences with students, and have them identify the verbs. Then have them say if the verb shows a physical action, mental action, or state of being.

      • Arturo cleans his kitchen. (cleans; physical action)
      • Emma often thinks about her trip to Brazil. (thinks; mental action)
      • After the soccer game, Alison was tired. (was; state of being)
