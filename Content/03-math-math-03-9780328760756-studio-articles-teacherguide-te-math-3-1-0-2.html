LESSON 2: How Many Minutes?

Lesson Overview

Watch a video about a class field trip. How long did students spend at Penguin Island? Use a number line for time, called a “timeline,” to find how many minutes have gone by.

Content Standards

3. MD—Measurement and Data

Solve problems involving measurement and estimation of intervals of time, liquid volumes, and masses of objects.

1. Tell and write time to the nearest minute and measure time intervals in minutes. Solve word problems involving addition and subtraction of time intervals in minutes, e.g., by representing the problem on a time line.

Key Concepts

Students solve problems involving the elapsed time between given start and end times. The elapsed times are multiples of 10 minutes or 5 minutes. The start and end times are purposefully not on different sides of an hour boundary in the first few problems. Students are not asked, for example, to find the elapsed time from 9:30 a.m. to 10:10 a.m., in which the start time is before 10:00 and the end time is after 10:00. Later in the lesson, students find elapsed time when the start and end times are on different sides of an hour boundary.

Students learn that a full turn of the minute hand is an hour (60 minutes) no matter where the minute hand starts. They also note that when the minute hand goes halfway around the clock, it is a half hour (30 minutes) no matter where the minute hand starts. The same understanding occurs with the relationship between a 1/4 turn and a quarter hour (15 minutes).

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Use a models and diagrams to find the length of a time interval in minutes

  • Solve problems involving elapsed time when the start and end time are given

Materials

  • Classroom analog clock, optional
