Task 6: Setting Reading Goals

    • Explain to students that setting and meeting reading goals are important to developing as a reader.

    • Have students Turn and Talk to a partner about the goals they set for themselves in second grade.

      Turn and Talks are quick questions or prompts that students talk about with a partner. They should turn to their partner, share their ideas, and listen to their partnerâ€™s ideas. When they hear the signal that time is up, they should turn their bodies back toward you, showing that they are ready to continue. Turn and Talks keep students engaged, help them to synthesize their thoughts, and allow you to check for comprehension.

      Guide students in selecting goals that are appropriate to their needs. For example, they may wish to read faster but need to monitor for meaning as they read because they struggle with comprehension, so this should be the goal selected.

    • Ask students to read the Possible Reading Goals list and choose three of the goals from the list (they may include the goals they set at the end of last year). Guide them to choose at least one goal for comprehension and one for vocabulary.

    • Tell them that they may add any goal they want to set that is not on the list.

    • Instruct them to record their three goals on the Reading Goals form.

    • When students are done, they should submit their form to you. If students are using a device, be sure they know how to do this. Reviewing these forms will help you get to know your students better.
