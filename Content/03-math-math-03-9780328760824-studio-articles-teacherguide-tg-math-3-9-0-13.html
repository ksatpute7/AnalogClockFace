Rectangle Patterns: Area

Answers

  1. The areas are 9 cm2, 12 cm2, 15 cm2, and 18 cm2.

  2. Each rectangle is 1 cm longer than the previous rectangle. The area increases by 3 cm2 for each successive rectangle, or each increase of 1 cm in length.

  3. The 8th rectangle will be 3 cm wide and 10 cm long with an area of 30 cm2.

  4. The areas are 20 cm2, 24 cm2, and 28 cm2.

  5. The rectangles again increase in length by 1 cm, but the area increases by 4 cm2 each time.

  6. If the length of the rectangle increases by 1 cm, the area will increase by the number of square centimeters that are the same as the width. In fact, for whatever amount the length increases, the area will increase by the increase in length multiplied by the width (which was 1 cm in the examples above).

Rectangle Patterns: Perimeter

Answers

  1. The perimeters are 12 cm, 14 cm, 16 cm, and 18 cm.

  2. Each rectangle is 1 cm longer than the previous rectangle. The perimeter increases by 2 cm for each successive rectangle, or each increase of 1 cm in length.

  3. The 8th rectangle will be 3 cm wide and 10 cm long with a perimeter of 26 cm.

  4. The perimeters are 18 cm, 20 cm, and 22 cm.

  5. The rectangles again increase in length by 1 cm, and the perimeter again increases by 2 cm each time.

  6. If the length of a rectangle increases by 1 cm, the perimeter will increase by 2 cm. In fact, for whatever amount the length increases, the perimeter will increase by two times that amount.
