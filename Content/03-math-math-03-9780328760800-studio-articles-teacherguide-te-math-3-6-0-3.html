LESSON 3: Multiplying by Ten

Lesson Overview

Students continue the practice of chanting the multiplication chants to attain fluency. Students solve a math story involving multiplication by 10 and share strategies for multiplying by 10. Next students hear three different strategies based on the properties. For each strategy they decide whether it will work, and they use it to calculate 10 × 9.

Content Standards

3.OA—Operations and Algebraic Thinking

Multiply and divide within 100.

7. Fluently multiply and divide within 100, using strategies such as the relationship between multiplication and division (e.g., knowing that 8 × 5 = 40, one knows 40 × 5 = 8) or properties of operations. By the end of Grade 3, know from memory all products of two one-digit numbers.

Key Concepts

Students with a good knowledge of place value will have little trouble understanding that 7 tens is 70. However, understanding that 10 sevens is also 70 is a bit more difficult. Students focus on applying strategies based on properties to help them understand multiplying by 10. Students draw on their experiences from earlier units and on their work from the beginning of this unit.

Using the properties, we might reason about 10 × 7 in these ways:

If you switch the order of the multiplier and the multiplicand, the product does not change. 10 × 7 = 7 × 10 and we know that 7 tens is 70.
You can break the multiplier into parts to carry out the multiplication without changing the product. 10 × 7 can be 5 × 7 + 5 × 7

10 × 7 can be 6 × 7 + 4 × 7 and so on
If you increase (or decrease) the multiplier by 1, the product will increase by the multiplicand. 10 × 7 = 9 × 7 + 7

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Understand and use strategies based on properties to multiply by 10
