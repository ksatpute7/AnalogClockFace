Task 2: Partner Reading

    • Though students have been working with partners throughout this unit, this task will help you to set up lasting quality partnerships in your classroom. All students can benefit from this instructional practice.

      Emerging P1.3.1

      Emerging ELLs can benefit from being partnered with other Emerging ELLs. These groups may need the support of one-on-one attention. Be sure to select a text that is at an independent reading level for the partnership.

      Expanding P1.3.1

      Expanding ELLs can benefit from being partnered with other Expanding ELLs. These students can follow the turn-taking protocol established during modeling. Be sure to select a text that is at an independent reading level for the partnership.

      Bridging P1.3.1

      Partner Bridging ELLs with other Bridging ELLs or native English speakers. These students can follow turn-taking protocols and sustained dialogue and build on each otherâ€™s responses. Be sure to select a text that is at an independent reading level for the partnership.

    • Choose a student to serve as your partner and demonstrate how you take turns reading the same text, discussing what you read, and then responding in writing using the Split-Page Notes.

    • Project the Split-Page Notes and personalize it for your reading to demonstrate how students can use the notes to help in their discussions.

    • Assign partners. Observe partnerships over the next few days to see if they are working. If not, make some changes.

    • Have students practice working together on a short piece of text. Have them reread letter E from What is a Community? from A to Z for this exercise. Observe as they take turns and then discuss the text.

      If partnerships are struggling to complete this task independently, pull them into a small group for direct instruction or guided practice.
