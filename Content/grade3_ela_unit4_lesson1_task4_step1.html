Identify Narrative Features

Share specific examples of the features that you found in the text.

As you learn about new features, add the definitions to your Vocabulary form.

Look through the text and your annotations to answer the following questions.

  • The setting is when and where a narrative takes place. What is the setting of Gram’s Angel Food Cake?
  • The narrator is the person telling the story. Who is the narrator in Gram’s Angel Food Cake? How do we know that the narrator is not Abi or Gram?
  • Characters are the people or animals in a narrative. Narratives often contain main (primary) and minor (secondary) characters. Main characters are the ones most important to the story, and they are the ones the action focuses on. Make a list of the characters in Gram’s Angel Food Cake. Put stars next to ones you think are main characters.
  • The events are the important actions that occur in a narrative and move the plot along. Make a list of the major events in the story. (Remember that you are not recording every single thing that happens in the story. Think about which events are major events and most important to the story.)
  • Many narratives contain a problem to be solved and a solution to that problem. What is the main problem or conflict in the narrative? How is it resolved?

Share your responses with the class. Your teacher will add your responses to the chart.
