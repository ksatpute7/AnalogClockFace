Task 5: Partner Discussion

    • Monitor the partner discussions to gauge the level of depth of the conversations and how closely students are reading their texts. This will help guide the annotations you model in coming lessons.

      Emerging P1.3.1

      Have Emerging ELLs use the pictures and the words on each page to share their book with a partner.

      Have them show and say the title of the book using this sentence frame:

      • The title of my book is _____.

      Have students lead a Picture Walk of their book to share what itâ€™s about using this sentence frame:

      • My book is about _____.

      Encourage them to use wh- questions to prompt their partners to share more information about the book (e.g., characters, events, and facts).

      Expanding P1.3.1

      Have Expanding ELLs state the title of their book and summarize what they have read so far. Encourage them to predict whether they will enjoy the book and why. Ask them to offer reasons why they would or would not like it using this sentence frame:

      • I think I will/will not like this book because _____.

      Bridging P1.3.1

      Have Bridging ELLs state the title of their book and summarize what they have read so far. Encourage them to predict whether they will enjoy the book and why. Ask them to offer reasons why they would or would not like it in a cohesive and in-depth manner. Encourage partners to offer feedback to one another based on what was shared.
