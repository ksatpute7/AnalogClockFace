Task 6: Make Connections

Lesson Guide

Have the students you identified share their work. Ask the class to consider the different patterns that the presenters used to complete the problems. Spend some time discussing errors so that everyone has the opportunity to learn from the mistakes. Encourage students to take notes about the different strategies that their classmates used to fill in the chart.

Ask students the following.

  • Do the numbers you wrote continue the patterns you found in the hundred chart?

  • How does the digit 0 help you read and write numbers?

  • What would happen if you did not use 0 as a placeholder in numbers like 160 and 200?

During the discussion, model reading three-digit numbers without saying “and” between the hundreds and the rest of the number (e.g., 104 is “one hundred four,” not “one hundred and four”).

Also have students share the “ask myself” questions they used, and any strategies they used if they got stuck. Add new ideas to the "Ask Myself" list in your classroom, or poster if you've made one.

If you have time, have a few students who completed the Challenge Problem present their work describing the mistakes that Zoe made.

ELL:

Be sure that all students (including ELLs) participate in the discussion, and monitor that ELLs do not shy away from this activity, as it is important that they share aloud so that they can hear their own voice and get used to talking in front of large groups. Encourage other students to be patient if the pace of ELLs is slower than native speakers, and explain that listening attentively is one way in which we show we care for others.

Mathematical Practices

Mathematical Practice 1: Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them.

  • Students discuss how they made sense of the problem by sharing their “ask myself” questions and describing any strategies they used to persevere if they got stuck.

Mathematical Practice 7: Look for and make use of structure.

  • Identifying the patterns in the chart helps students understand written numbers in our place value system.
