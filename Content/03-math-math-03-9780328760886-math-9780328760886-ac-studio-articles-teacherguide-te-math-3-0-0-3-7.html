Task 7: What Is a Good Summary?

Lesson Guide

Begin this task with a brief discussion about the qualities of a good summary.

  • It focuses on the important mathematics in the lesson.

  • It uses a few sentences to explain the mathematics.

  • It goes beyond providing answers and focuses on the bigger ideas in the lesson.

Have each student write a summary about how to write missing numbers in the number chart. Then have students work together to write a class summary. If the resulting class summary is helpful, share it with the students.

ELL:

A Possible Summary

Patterns can help you make sense of mathematics. The digit in the hundreds place counts the hundreds, the digit in the tens place counts the tens, and the digit in the ones place counts the ones. In the numbers chart you add 1 as you move right along each row and add 10 as you go down the columns.

Additional Discussion Points

There are additional things you might want to discuss.

  • Subtract 1 to the left along the row and subtract 10 as you go up the column.

  • The columns in these numbers charts show skip-counting by 10s.
