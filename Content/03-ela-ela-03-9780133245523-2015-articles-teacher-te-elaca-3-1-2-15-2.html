Task 2: Jobs Discussion

    • Have students meet in groups of four to discuss what they found the most interesting or surprising about the people they interviewed. Allow enough time for each student in the group to have a turn to share.

      Emerging P1.3.1

      Define and give an example of the words “interesting” and “surprising.” Point out to Spanish-speaking Emerging ELLs that the word interesting has the Spanish cognate interesante. Support students as they contribute to the conversation by providing sentence frames that are easily accessible from where the students are seated.

      • I interviewed _____.
      • The most interesting thing about his or her job was _____.
      • The most surprising thing about his or her job was _____.

      Expanding P1.3.1

      Ask an Expanding ELL to define the words “interesting” and “surprising” to ensure students understand their meaning. Support students as they follow turn-taking rules during the conversation by providing sentence frames that encourage them to speak in expanded sentences.

      • I interviewed _____.
      • The most interesting thing about his or her job was _____ because _____.
      • The most surprising thing about his or her job was _____ because _____.

      Bridging P1.3.1

      Pair Bridging ELL partnerships with other ELL or non-ELL partnerships. Encourage students to engage in conversations using a variety of sentences.

    • Monitor the discussions and choose a few of the interesting and surprising facts to share with the whole group.
