Day 3: Open Sort

  • Review the words with the students. In the review, focus on patterns for making words plural and word meaning. Define or have students define each word to be sure they are clear on the meaning.

    • bodies
    • bunches
    • copies
    • crashes
    • families
    • glasses
    • inches
    • lists
    • parties
    • pencils
    • pennies
    • plants
    • pockets
    • supplies
    • wishes

  • While the goal is for students to identify the patterns in the spelling words (in this case, plurals), they should feel free to sort any way they wish, using between two and five categories. Use the teacher resource Sample Open Sort to give them some ideas on how to do this.

  • Remind students that previously, they began looking at the words and noticing what makes them similar and different. Now have them continue to work with the spelling pattern of the week by completing an Open Sort. This is an opportunity to begin exploring and categorizing the words in the sort by creating groups of words using the columns.

  • Have students sort their words and then work with a partner to discover and discuss the patterns they created.

  • Monitor student work; pull small groups who may need additional support in seeing the pattern.

  • Have a few students share one of their groupings and the generalization they made about the words.
