LESSON 8: Equivalent Fractions

Lesson Overview

Use number lines and area models to help you understand and identify equivalent fractions.

Content Standards

3.NF—Number and Operations—Fractions

Develop understanding of fractions as numbers.

  1. 3. Explain equivalence of fractions in special cases, and compare fractions by reasoning about their size.

    1. Understand two fractions as equivalent (=) if they are the same size, or the same point on a number line.

    2. Recognize and generate simple equivalent fractions, e.g., 12=24 and 23=46. Explain why the fractions are equivalent, e.g., by using a visual fraction model.

    3. Express whole numbers as fractions, and recognize fractions that are equivalent to whole numbers. Examples: Express 3 in the form 3=31; recognize that 61=6; locate 44 and 1 at the same point of a number line diagram.

Key Concepts

Equivalent fractions are fractions that represent the same quantity, or the same part of 1. These area models show that 12, 24, and 48 are equivalent because each represents the same part of 1 whole.

This equivalence can also be seen on number lines. Below, the numbers 12, 24, and 48 are all the same distance from 0.

Students find equivalent fractions using area models and number lines. In Grades 4 and 5, they will see that they can generate equivalent fractions by multiplying or dividing the numerator and the denominator of a fraction by the same number.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Understand what it means for fractions to be equivalent.

  • Use number lines or area models to find equivalent fractions.
