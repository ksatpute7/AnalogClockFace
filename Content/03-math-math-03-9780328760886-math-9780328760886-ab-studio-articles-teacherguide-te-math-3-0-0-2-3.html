Task 3: What Is a Good Presentation?

Lesson Guide

Begin by reminding students about the work they did on expanded form in Lesson 1. Chan and Zoe’s presentations share their work on that problem.

Refer students to the image of Chan and Zoe’s student presentations.

Have a couple students read Chan and Zoe’s presentation aloud.

Ask students to compare the presentations.

  • Do you think one of these presentations is better than the other? Why?

  • How is the work organized?

  • What makes the students’ thinking clear?

  • Why are pictures helpful?

Think: Have students spend a couple minutes thinking about these three questions on their own. Consider providing the following sentence frames on the board for students.

  • _______’s work is organized by_______.

  • _______’s work is more clear than_______’s work because_______.

  • Pictures are helpful in_______’s work because_______.

Turn and Talk: Tell students to turn and talk to their partner about what they think is good about one of the presentations.

Discuss: After, have students share their ideas as a class. Record students’ suggestions on chart paper in a permanent location of the classroom.

Point out that there are two “steps” to presentations: preparing them and presenting them. Ask students the following question.

  • How can you give a good presentation?

These Hints are given to students as ideas about how to prepare clear presentations.

  • Understand your own work.

  • Use visuals and gestures to help others know what you are talking about.

  • Say fewer words and choose them carefully.

  • Speak clearly and slowly.

  • Stay calm. Remember to breathe.

  • Listen carefully to comments and questions.

  • Pause to think about your response before speaking.

Add students’ ideas to the chart of what makes a good presentation. After students finish sharing their ideas, review each suggestion for clarity. It is not necessary that students agree on each suggestion; diversity in presentation styles can be beneficial. Take a picture of the students’ list, and share it with the students.
