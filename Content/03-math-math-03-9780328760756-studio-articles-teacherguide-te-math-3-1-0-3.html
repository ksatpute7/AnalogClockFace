LESSON 3: How Many Minutes Elapsed?

Lesson Overview

Solve more problems about elapsed time. Learn different ways to find out how much time has gone by.

Content Standards

3. MD—Measurement and Data

Solve problems involving measurement and estimation of intervals of time, liquid volumes, and masses of objects.

1. Tell and write time to the nearest minute and measure time intervals in minutes. Solve word problems involving addition and subtraction of time intervals in minutes, e.g., by representing the problem on a time line.

Key Concepts

Students continue to solve elapsed time problems. The timeline and the model analog clock help students conceptualize the time interval in parts. Students are not expected to use a subtraction computation. Note that a subtraction computation can be used when the time interval is within an hour, such as from 11:10 to 11:45:

45 min−10 min=35 min

But a subtraction computation will not work for problems where the elapsed time crosses an hour boundary, such as from 11:45 to 12:10. Neither of these equations makes sense as a representation of the problem, nor will they produce the correct result:

10 min−45 min=?

45 min−10 min=35 min

Now students begin to think of the elapsed time in parts: from 11:45 to 12:00 is 15 minutes and from 12:00 to 12:10 is 10 minutes:

15 min+10 min=25 min

They can easily calculate the total elapsed time using mental calculations. They come to recognize what a time interval represents on the clock face (e.g., the distance between two big tick marks is 5 minutes).

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Use a models and diagrams to find the length of a time interval in minutes

  • Solve problems involving elapsed time when the start and end time are given

Materials

  • Classroom analog clock, optional
