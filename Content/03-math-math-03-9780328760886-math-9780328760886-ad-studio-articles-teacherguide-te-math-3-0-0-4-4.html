Task 4: Patterns in Skip-Counting by 5s and 10s

Lesson Guide

Read the directions to the problem to the class. Point out that students can choose whether to skip-count by 5s or 10s. Remind students to tap to shade in the numbers as they say them.

Have students work individually to skip-count and describe the patterns they see. Then have them discuss their work with their partner. Tell partners to focus on explaining to each other the patterns they noticed in the chart.

Remind students to be good listeners and ask questions if they are not clear about their partner’s explanations.

Mathematical Practices

Mathematical Practice 3: Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others

  • Students’ primary task in this problem is to explain the patterns they used as they skip-counted. To construct a cohesive argument, they need to make conjectures and follow the logic to verify the truth of their conjectures.

Mathematical Practice 5: Use appropriate tools strategically

  • Students use the Hundred Chart tool to enhance their understanding of place value.

Mathematical Practice 7: Look for and make use of structure

  • Students describe the patterns that occur when skip-counting by 5s and 10s.

Interventions

Student gets stuck on the problem.

  • What part of the problem are you having difficulty with?

  • Can you use any of the ideas on the What to Do if You Get Stuck list to help you?

Student is skip-counting by 5s but has only shaded one column.

  • What is the pattern in the columns of the number chart?

  • Did you shade every number that you said while skip-counting by 5s?

  • Why do you need to shade two columns to show skip-counting by 5s?

Student shades a whole row in the number chart.

  • What is the pattern in the rows of the number chart?

  • Did you shade any numbers that you did not say while skip-counting by?

  • Why do you need to shade a column when skip-counting by 5s or 10s?
