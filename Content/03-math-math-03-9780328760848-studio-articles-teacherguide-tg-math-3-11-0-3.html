LESSON 3: Solving Volume Problems

Lesson Overview

Students use addition and subtraction to solve word problems involving volume.

Add, subtract, multiply, or divide to solve one-step word problems involving masses or volumes that are given in the same units, e.g., by using drawings (such as a beaker with a measurement scale) to represent the problem.

Key Concepts

Beakers and other measuring devices have scale markings that assign a numeric value to an amount of liquid. This numeric value has meaning as a measurement unit. The scale is a type of number line, with marks indicating equivalent intervals that match to a consistent measurement unit. In this lesson students solve word problems about volume using visual representations that have direct real-world meaning.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Solve word problems involving measuring volume using addition and subtraction.
