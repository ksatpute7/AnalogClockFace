Work Time

Task 3: Practice With Conjunctions

    • Explain that today students will work in pairs putting sentences together using the following conjunctions, written on the board: after, because, when, before, and if.

      Emerging P2.3.7

      Emerging ELLs should practice combining clauses in simple ways; therefore, students should only complete the examples that do not require changing the order of the sentences. Prompt students to reference the Conjunctions chart if they are confused. Have students complete this activity in a small group with your support.

      Expanding P2.3.7

      Expanding ELLs should combine clauses in several ways, including expressing cause and effect. Remind students that they may need to change the order of the clauses for the sentence to express cause and effect or concession. If students are confused, encourage them to reference the Conjunctions chart. Students should complete this activity in partnerships with other ELLs or with students who have a higher level of English proficiency.

      Bridging P2.3.7

      Bridging ELLs should combine clauses in a wide variety of ways. Allow students to reference the Conjunctions chart if needed. Students can complete this activity independently or with the support of a peer.

    • Tell students to combine the following pairs of sentences using a different conjunction each time. Remind them that they may change the order of the sentences to stress the most important sentence of the pair.

    • Model the following example: Rafe gets up at 6 every morning. Rafe brushes his teeth.

      • When Rafe gets up at 6 every morning, he brushes his teeth.
      • After Rafe gets up at 6 every morning, he brushes his teeth.
      • Rafe brushes his teeth after he gets up.

    • Note for the students that it is all right to make a few changes if the meaning remains clear.

    • Monitor the pairs as they work to assess their understanding and to address misunderstandings as they arise for pairs or for the whole class.

    • Ask several pairs to share examples for each sentence pair. Ask students how the meaning changes as they use different conjunctions and/or a different sentence order for the same pair.
