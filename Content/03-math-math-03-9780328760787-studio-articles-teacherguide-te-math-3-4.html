UNIT 4

Explore 2-D Figures

Type of Unit: Concept

Length of Unit: 10 days

Prior Knowledge

Students should be able to:

  • Recognize two-dimensional (2-D) shapes: quadrilaterals, squares, and rectangles

  • Understand units of length, finding differences in lengths, and adding lengths

Lesson Flow

After an initial exploratory lesson that reminds students of the definition and characteristics of a quadrilateral, the unit focuses on characteristics of different quadrilaterals: rectangles, squares, and rhombuses.

Students know from second grade that rectangles have four right angles. In this unit, students refine their definition of rectangles to include having opposite sides of the same length. This definition is applied to squares, and students discover that squares are rectangles.

In second grade students learned how to find the perimeter of shapes. In this unit, they use the knowledge that opposite sides (or all four sides) of a rectangle are the same length to find the perimeter of rectangles.

Lastly, students are introduced to rhombuses and find that a square is a rhombus. Students also calculate perimeter for rhombuses. There is a two-day Gallery, where students investigate other topics related to quadrilaterals. The unit ends with a Unit Assessment.

Contents

To navigate this document, click below on any of the lesson titles.

  1. Unit Overview 4

  1. Lesson 1: What Is a Quadrilateral? 5

    Students will explore attributes of quadrilaterals.

  2. Lesson 2: Rectangles 15

    Students will construct and define rectangles.

  3. Lesson 3: Rectangles and Squares 22

    Students will construct and define squares. They will explore how squares are related to rectangles, and they will build rectangles to meet specific conditions.

  4. Lesson 4: Perimeter 28

    Students will devise methods for finding the perimeter of a rectangle based on characteristics of rectangles (and squares). They will draw rectangles for a given perimeter.

  5. Lesson 5: Rhombuses and Squares 51

    Students will define a rhombus. They will explore how rhombuses are related to squares, and they will find the perimeter of rhombuses.

  6. Lesson 6: Building Quadrilaterals 44

    Students will combine shapes to compose rectangles, squares, and rhombuses. Then students will decompose their shapes into two triangles. For the Self Check, students will draw rectangles with a given perimeter.

  7. Lesson 7: Putting It Together 54

    Students will revise their work on the Self Check task. Then they will critique sample student work.

  8. Lessons 8–9: Gallery 60

    Students who have a clear understanding of the content thus far in the unit will work on Gallery problems of their choosing.

  9. Lesson 10: Unit Assessment X

    Students will take the Unit Assessment to find out what they have learned.

Common Core State Standards for Mathematics

This unit focuses on the following Common Core State Standards for Mathematics:

3.MD—Measurement and Data

Geometric measurement: recognize perimeter as an attribute of plane figures and distinguish between linear and area measures.

8. Solve real world and mathematical problems involving perimeters of polygons, including finding the perimeter given the side lengths, finding an unknown side length, and exhibiting rectangles with the same perimeter and different areas or with the same area and different perimeters.

3.G—Geometry

Reason with shapes and their attributes.

1. Understand that shapes in different categories (e.g., rhombuses, rectangles, and others) may share attributes (e.g., having four sides), and that the shared attributes can define a larger category (e.g., quadrilaterals). Recognize rhombuses, rectangles, and squares as examples of quadrilaterals, and draw examples of quadrilaterals that do not belong to any of these subcategories.

Mathematical Practices

  1. Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them.

  2. Reason abstractly and quantitatively.

  3. Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others.

  4. Model with mathematics.

  5. Use appropriate tools strategically.

  6. Attend to precision.

  7. Look for and make use of structure.

  8. Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning.
