Task 1: Participate in Mathematical Discussions

Lesson Guide

Explain to students that during mathematical discussions, they will sometimes present their work to their classmates, while other times they will listen to their classmates present. Point out that how the listeners participate is as important for a good mathematical discussion as how the presenters participate.

Ask students the following questions.

  • How can listeners contribute during presentations to help everyone learn?

Have a student read aloud the three things that students should do when they are listening to a presenter.

Ask students if they noticed that in previous lesson during Ways of Thinking they were given Hints about the kinds of questions to ask presenters. Explain that students can use these Ways of Thinking Hints to ask meaningful questions; they can also think of their own questions to ask. Ask students the following question.

  • What are some of the general types of questions that you can ask presenters?

Think: Have students think about this question for a moment.

Turn and Talk: Ask students to discuss their ideas with their partner.

Discuss: Ask students share their ideas with the class. Record students’ questions on a chart that you will keep posted throughout the year. Review each question for clarity. Take a photo and share it with the students.

These Hints are given to students as ideas about questions that they can ask presenters.

  • “I do not understand. What do you mean by ...?”

  • “How do you know that ...?”

  • “What you said is like... .”

  • “Is that different from ...?”

  • “Is your conclusion always true for...?”

Other questions that students suggest might fit within these categories:

  • Clarify—Tell the presenter when you do not understand.

  • Critique—Challenge reasoning that is flawed.

  • Connect—Explore how different strategies result in the same answer.

  • Compare—Describe similarities and differences.

  • Notice Structure—Ask whether a conclusion is always, sometimes, or never true.

ELL:

Monitor that ELLs understand the meaning of each of these questions. If there are concerns, explain them one by one.
