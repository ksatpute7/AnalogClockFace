Task 6: Sentence Deconstruction

    • Read the sentence aloud.

    • Then ask how many ideas are in this sentence. (2) Have students identify the ideas. (Some communities grow their own food. People in most communities buy their food in stores.)

    • Ask students:

      • How are these two ideas are related?

        Help them to see that they are contrasting sentences; some communities grow their own food but most buy their food.

    • Ask the following question:

      • What word signaled that a contrasting idea is in the sentence? (but)

    • Offer this sentence as another example of the use of the signal word but:

      • Some people like chocolate ice cream, but I prefer vanilla.
