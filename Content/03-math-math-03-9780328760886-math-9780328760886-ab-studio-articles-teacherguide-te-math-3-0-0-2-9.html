Task 9: Describe Expanded Form

Lesson Guide

Tell students that after they share and discuss their work during Ways of Thinking, in most lessons they will then summarize what they have learned. Students will either write a summary individually or read and discuss a given summary as a class. Today students will read and discuss a summary together.

Mathematics

Give partners a few minutes to read and discuss the summary. Then ask the class the following questions.

  • Does the amount change if you take away 1 hundred and add 10 tens?

  • Suppose one number has 2 hundreds, 5 tens, and 3 ones. Another number has 1 hundred, 15 tens, and 3 ones. Are these numbers equal?

  • Are there other things that might be useful to add to this summary?

As a class, add any additional details that students think are important. When you are finished, share the class summary with the students. Tell students that they can refer to the summary in future lessons if they need to review the mathematical concepts of todayâ€™s lesson.
