LESSON 6: Building Quadrilaterals

Lesson Overview

Students will combine triangles and rectangles to form different quadrilaterals. Then, students will complete a Self Check to find out what they have learned about quadrilaterals.

Understand that shapes in different categories (e.g., rhombuses, rectangles, and others) may share attributes (e.g., having four sides), and that the shared attributes can define a larger category (e.g., quadrilaterals). Recognize rhombuses, rectangles, and squares as examples of quadrilaterals, and draw examples of quadrilaterals that do not belong to any of these subcategories.

Key Concepts

Students use a collection of triangles and rectangles to compose different quadrilaterals.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Understand that rectangles, squares, and rhombuses can be composed of triangles and/or rectangles.

  • Construct rectangles, squares, and rhombuses from given shapes.
