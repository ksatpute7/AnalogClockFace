Task 3: Rosie’s Community Working Together

    • Make sure students understand the word achieve.

    • Have students answer the writing prompt in their Notebook.

    • Circulate through the room and provide help when needed.

      Emerging P1.3.10b

      Have Emerging ELLs recount what Rosie’s community did by rereading the text and looking at the pictures. Students can plan their ideas by writing notes about what Rosie’s community did. Provide a sentence frame to help students answer the question:

      • Rosie got more things to send to Katje by _____.

      Expanding P1.3.10b

      Have Expanding ELLs recount what Rosie’s community did in complete sentences. Pair Expanding ELLs with students who have a higher level of English proficiency. Provide a sentence frame to answer the question:

      • Rosie’s community achieved more by working together. They achieved _____.

      Bridging P1.3.10b

      Bridging ELLs do not need to plan responses, unless you deem it necessary for an individual student. Monitor students as they write their responses to be sure they understand the question. Clarify the word “achieve” if necessary. Students should include increasingly detailed sentences and key words from the text.

    • Support students who find the task challenging by directing them back to the text. Consider pulling a group of students who need support in finding the information in the text to work on the task together.

    • This is an inferential question, so students will not find the answer right there in the book. Students will need to find examples of Rosie’s community’s behavior and then decide how they achieved more by working together.
