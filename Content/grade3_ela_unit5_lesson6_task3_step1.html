Grandmother Spider Steals the Sun

Discuss the following questions with a partner.

  • Where in the world was the Anansi story created?

  • What part of the natural world did it explain?

Follow along as your teacher reads the Cherokee story "Grandmother Spider Steals the Sun." Listen closely so you understand what the story is about.

As you listen, think about the storyteller you watched in the last lesson. How does your teacher’s storytelling compare?

Then discuss the story with your classmates:

  • What happened in the story?
  • Why does the story begin in only blackness?
  • Why did it take several attempts to get the sun to the other half of the world?
  • Do you think that the animals were selfish to steal the sun from the other side of the world?
  • Were they greedy?
  • Do you think there were consequences for attempting to steal the sun?

Be sure you can support your responses with evidence from the story.
