LESSON 2: Addition and Subtraction

Lesson Overview

In this problem-solving lesson, students will explore the connections between addition and subtraction as they solve word problems. They will use tape diagrams and equations to help them understand and represent the inverse relationship between these two operations.

Content Standards

3.OA—Operations and Algebraic Thinking

Solve problems involving the four operations, and identify and explain patterns in arithmetic.

8. Solve two-step word problems using the four operations. Represent these problems using equations with a letter standing for the unknown quantity. Assess the reasonableness of answers using mental computation and estimation strategies including rounding.

Key Concepts

In solving addition and subtraction word problems, students will see that the same diagram can represent both operations. For example:

This diagram combines parts with values 18 and 24 to get a total value of 42. So, it represents the addition equations 18 + 24 = 42 or 24 + 18 = 42. But this model can also be interpreted as representing subtraction. Specifically, it shows that taking away a part of value 18 from a total of 42 leaves a part with value 24, or 42 – 18 = 24. It also shows that taking away a part of value 24 from a total of 42 leaves a part with value 18, or 42 – 24 = 18.

Students should recognize that the addition problem can be written as 18 + 24 = 42 or 24 + 18 = 42 due to the commutative property of addition. However, when writing the subtraction problems, students must write the minuend (42) as the first value in the number sentence.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Identify and communicate connections between addition and subtraction

  • Model word problems using tape diagrams

  • Model word problems using expressions and equations
