Task 2: Conjunctions

    • Put two objects such as an apple and a banana on the desk.

    • Ask students how they could put the following sentences together:

      • I have an apple. I have a banana.

    • Tell them and is a conjunction. Display the word conjunction; write and under it.

    • Ask them how to put the following sentences together:

      • I like apples. I like bananas better.

    • Tell them but is also a conjunction and write but under conjunction.

    • Ask them what conjunction would fit the following sentence:

      • I would like either an apple _____ a banana.

    • Tell them or is also a conjunction and write or under conjunction.

    • Tell students there are other kinds of conjunctions that can combine sentences and parts of sentences.

    • Ask pairs of students to combine the following two sentences without using and, or, or but:

      • I finished my homework.
      • I got to watch television.

    • Monitor their work, and as they come up with various subordinating conjunctions, such as after, because, when, and before, write these on the board in a separate list under conjunctions.

    • Ask pairs that came up with different conjunctions to share their sentences, and as they do, refer to the words under conjunctions.

    • Ask how the order of these two sentences could be reversed using any of these conjunctions, such as “I got to watch television because _____.”

    • Tell students that writers try a different order so that the most important idea, usually at the end of the sentence, stands out and the writer’s meaning is clear.

    • Display this sentence from What is a Community? from A to Z: “Many communities are interconnected, or joined to one another, because people know what other people in faraway places are doing.” (This was the sentence used in Lesson 15’s deconstruction task.) Ask students to find the two conjunctions in this sentence (or, because) and to discuss the function of each. (Or connects interconnected to its meaning; because connects the first part of the sentence to the second, providing a reason communities can be interconnected.)
