Opening

Task 1: Vocabulary

    • Use the following questions and discussion points below to introduce two words students will encounter in their reading: ensure and resemble.

    • Ask students:

      • What word can you see inside the word ensure? (sure)
      • Tell them: To be sure means to be certain. To ensure is to make sure something happens.
      • Give this example from the Common Read: “Laws are rules that protect people in a community from crime. Laws also ensure that people are treated fairly.”
      • Ask the class: How can laws ensure that you are treated fairly?

    • Ask students:

      • What does it mean when a person says you resemble someone in your family? (It means you look like them.)
      • Tell them: We can use the word resemble in other ways, as well. In your reading, you will see this sentence: “Many communities have zoos where animals live in areas that resemble their natural environment.” Have students say what the word resemble in the sentence means. (It means that the animals live in places that look like where they used to live.)

    • Say to students:

      • We can use the word resemble in other ways, as well. In your reading, you will see this sentence: “Many communities have zoos where animals live in areas that resemble their natural environment.”
      • Have students say what the word resemble in the sentence means. (It means that the animals live in places that look like where they used to live.)

    • Have students enter these two new words in their Personal Notebook and write a simple definition for each.
