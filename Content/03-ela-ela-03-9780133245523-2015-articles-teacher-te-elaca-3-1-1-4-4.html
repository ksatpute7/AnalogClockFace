Closing

Task 4: Community Reflection

    • Support the discussion by asking questions such as:

      • In your own words, what is a rubric? Review the definition of this word if students are unclear about what a rubric is.
      • What does the rubric allow us to do that we couldn’t do on our own?
      • Are these rules? Do all communities have rules?
      • Do we fit the definition of a community? Why or why not?

      Emerging P1.3.11

      Create sentence frames to help Emerging ELLs support opinions and responses with one piece of relevant information. For example:

      • The rubric helps/does not help me because _____.
      • We are/aren’t a community of readers because _____.

      Expanding P1.3.11

      Create sentence frames to help Expanding ELLs provide good reasons and to increase details when speaking. Students should have two pieces of relevant information. For example:

      • The rubric helps/does not help me because _____.
      • We are/aren’t a community of readers because _____.
      • These are rules since _____.

      Bridging P1.3.11

      Create sentence frames to help Bridging ELLs provide good reasons to persuade others while speaking. Students should have three pieces of relevant information. For example:

      • The rubric helps/does not help me because _____.
      • We are/aren’t a community of readers because _____.
      • These are rules since _____.
      • We do/do not fit the definition of community because _____.
