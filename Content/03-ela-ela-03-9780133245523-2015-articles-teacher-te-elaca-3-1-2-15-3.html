Work Time

Task 3: Writing Conferences

    • Tell students that during Work Time you will be “conferring” or meeting with them individually to discuss their writing. Explain that Writing Conferences are very similar to Reading Conferences in that they are one-to-one meetings between a student and teacher, and that during the conference you (the teacher) can individualize instruction and support each student.

      Make sure to confer with any of your struggling writers who may be behind so that they can catch up to the rest of the class. If needed, invite your professional supporting SWDs to meet with any students who need help finishing. Share your notes on the student’s writing and the Report rubric so that the professional has a firm idea of what has been done and where to go next.

      It is important to establish the ritual of conferring with students about their writing.

    • Make a conference schedule, but be flexible, as students not on the schedule may need your support.

      Emerging P1.3.5

      For Emerging ELLs, make the conference note very specific and write it in short, simple phrases. During the conference, model how to practice that particular strategy. Ask yes-no and wh- questions to ensure students understand the steps they need to follow.

      Expanding P1.3.5

      For Expanding ELLs, write the conference note in complete basic sentences. During the conference, model how to practice that particular strategy. Ask probing questions to ensure students understand the steps they need to follow.

      Bridging P1.3.5

      The amplification in the Expanding level also applies for this level.

    • Use an anecdotal form for recording conference notes so that you and your students understand individual expectations and instructions. This form is a place for you to record and monitor instruction and student work as well as to plan instruction for future lessons and conferences.
