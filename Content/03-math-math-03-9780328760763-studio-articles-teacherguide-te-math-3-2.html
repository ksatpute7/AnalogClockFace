Understand Numbers

Type of Unit: Modeling

Length of Unit: 10 days

Prior Knowledge

Students should be able to:

  • Understand place value for numbers up to 1,000.

  • Add and subtract numbers with totals to 999.

  • Understand the meaning of addition and subtraction.

  • Understand that ten 100s equal one 1,000.

Lesson Flow

In the first two lessons of this unit, students look for patterns in an addition table. Students use patterns in the table to learn about these properties:

  • Compensation Strategy: If you increase one addend and decrease the other addend by the same quantity, the sum does not change.

  • Constant Difference Strategy: If you add (or subtract) the same number from the minuend and the subtrahend, the difference stays the same.

Students then use the properties as strategies for addition and subtraction of larger numbers.

In the third lesson, students build on their previous understanding of hundreds, tens, and ones to round numbers to the nearest hundred and the nearest ten.

In the rest of the unit, students extend their knowledge of adding numbers up to 999 to add to 1,000 and extend their knowledge of subtracting from numbers up to 999 to subtracting from 1,000.

  1. Lesson 1: Patterns in the Addition Table 6

    Students will explore patterns in the addition table and relate one pattern to a mental-addition strategy.

  2. Lesson 2: More Patterns 17

    Students will use a pattern in the addition table to develop the constant-difference strategy for subtraction. Then, students will use the idea of turn-arounds to fill in empty cells in the addition table.

  3. Lesson 3: Rounding 30

    Students will use an interactive number line to help you round numbers to the nearest ten or nearest hundred.

  4. Lesson 4: Adding Large Numbers 39

    Students will solve a problem that requires you to add two 3-digit numbers with a sum in the thousands. Then students will practice adding 3-digit numbers.

  5. Lesson 5: Subtracting Large Numbers 48

    Students will solve problems that require you to subtract a 3-digit number from a number in the thousands.

  6. Lesson 6: Subtracting From 1,000 58

    Students will explore methods of subtracting a whole number from 1,000. Students will complete a Self Check task.

  7. Lesson 7: Putting It Together 71

    Students will review and revise your work on the Self Check task. Students will also critique student work on rounding adding, and subtracting whole numbers.

  8. Lessons 8–9: Gallery 79

    Students who have a clear understanding of the content thus far in the unit will work on Gallery problems of their choosing.

  9. Lesson 10: Unit Assessment 82

    The final assessment is computer scored items and performance tasks that tests students on rounding, adding and subtracting

Common Core State Standards for Mathematics

This unit focuses on the following Common Core State Standards for Mathematics:

3.OA—Operations and Algebraic Thinking

Solve problems involving the four operations, and identify and explain patterns in arithmetic.

  • 9. Identify arithmetic patterns (including patterns in the addition table or multiplication table), and explain them using properties of operations. For example, observe that 4 times a number is always even, and explain why 4 times a number can be decomposed into two equal addends.

3.NBT—Number and Operations in Base Ten

Use place value understanding and properties of operations to perform multi-digit arithmetic.

  • 1. Use place value understanding to round whole numbers to the nearest 10 or 100.

  • 2. Fluently add and subtract within 1000 using strategies and algorithms based on place value, properties of operations, and/or the relationship between addition and subtraction.

Mathematical Practices

  1. Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them.

  2. Reason abstractly and quantitatively.

  3. Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others.

  4. Model with mathematics.

  5. Use appropriate tools strategically.

  6. Attend to precision.

  7. Look for and make use of structure.

  8. Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning.
