Task 4: Grammar and Language: Regular and Irregular Plural Nouns

    • Explain to students that most nouns in English simply add an -s to form the plural.

    • However, as they have seen in words that end in -ch, -sh, and -ss, add -es. Point out that words that end in -x and -zz can also be added to this list.

    • Point out that they also learned that words that end in a consonant + y (like family and body) change the -y to -i and add -es.

    • Say, “When words follow a particular rule, we call them regular. However, there are nouns in English that do not follow these rules, and we call these nouns irregular.”

    • Discuss these irregular nouns with students: man/men; woman/women; foot/feet; child/children; mouse/mice; goose/geese; tooth/teeth. Point out that since these nouns don’t follow rules, the plural form of irregular nouns needs to be memorized.

    • Ask students to choose any four pages from the Common Read and list all the plural nouns they find. They can work with a partner on this task. Then discuss how most of the plural nouns were formed. (Most of the nouns in the book added -s to form the plural.)
