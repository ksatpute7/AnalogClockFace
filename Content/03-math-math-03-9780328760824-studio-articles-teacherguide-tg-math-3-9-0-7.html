LESSON 7: Area of Rectilinear Figures

Lesson Overview

Students will explore strategies for finding the area of rectilinear figures.

Content Standards

3.MD—Measurement and Data

Geometric measurement: understand concepts of area and relate area to multiplication and addition.

  • 7. Relate area to the operations of multiplication and addition.

    • b. Multiply side lengths to find areas of rectangles with whole-number side lengths in the context of solving real world and mathematical problems, and represent whole-number products as rectangular areas in mathematical reasoning

    • c. Use tiling to show in a concrete case that the area of a rectangle with whole-number side lengths a and b+c is the sum of a×b and a×c . Use area models to represent the distributive property in mathematical reasoning.

    • d. Recognize area as additive. Find areas of rectilinear figures by decomposing them into non-overlapping rectangles and adding the areas of the non-overlapping parts, applying this technique to solve real world problems.

Key Concepts

There are several strategies that can be used to find the area of rectilinear figures such as this:

  • Find the area of a 4 cm × 5 cm rectangle then subtract the unneeded area.

  • Divide the figure into rectangles: Find the area of each rectangle and add the areas.

    4 cm×3 cm+3 cm×2 cm 12  cm 2 +6  cm 2 =18  cm 2

  • Change the figure so that it becomes a rectangle: Find the area of the rectangle.

    3 cm×(5 cm+1 cm) 3 cm×6 cm=18  cm 2

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Find the area of rectilinear figures.
