LESSON 4: So Heavy!

Lesson Overview

Students compare the weights of common objects. They first compare the weight of objects by feel alone, and then build and use a homemade balance to compare weights.

Content Standards

3.MD—Measurement and Data

Solve problems involving measurement and estimation of intervals of time, liquid volumes, and masses of objects.

  • 2. Measure and estimate liquid volumes and masses of objects using standard units of grams (g), kilograms (kg), and liters (l). Add, subtract, multiply, or divide to solve one-step word problems involving masses or volumes that are given in the same units, e.g., by using drawings (such as a beaker with a measurement scale) to represent the problem.

Key Concepts

Weight and mass are attributes of a physical object that can be measured and assigned a numeric value. Comparing the weights of objects provides students with concrete experience in determining relative magnitudes—in this case between the weights of four different objects.

Goals and Learning Objectives

  • Devise a strategy for determining the relative weights of a set of objects by feel.

  • Devise a strategy for determining the relative weights of a set of objects by using a homemade balance.

  • Develop a rudimentary understanding of how to compare weights.

Materials

  • Selection of objects to weigh, including some objects that are close in size but have significantly different masses, and including at least four objects per group of four students that will fit in the cups of the homemade balance (see below); possible items include books, paper clips, pieces of paper, coins, blocks, pens, pencils, tennis balls, ping-pong balls, rocks (each group of four students will choose four objects)

  • Materials to create a homemade balance such as the one shown—string, ruler, binder clip, two paper clips, two plastic cups (one set of materials for each group of four students, and one set of materials for you to use to demonstrate how to make the balance)

Preparation

Poke two holes in each plastic cup for the string to go through (see balance image).
