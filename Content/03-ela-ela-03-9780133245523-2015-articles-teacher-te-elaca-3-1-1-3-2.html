Task 2: Genre Sort

    • Show students the Genre Form and tell them they are going to pretend to be librarians who have to sort different books into their correct genres. There are 20 descriptions total; consider having students complete 15 out of the 20.

      Emerging P1.3.6

      Provide Emerging ELLs with the Genre Form, but ask them to classify only 7 of the 20 books on the list. Have students match the front covers with the title and the genre description. Ask students to describe the selected genres.

      Expanding P1.3.6

      Provide Expanding ELLs with the Genre Form, but ask them to classify only 10 of the 20 books on the list. Have students match a front cover to each genre description. Ask students to describe the ideas in detail based on their understanding of the genres.

      Bridging P1.3.6

      Provide Bridging ELLs with the Genre Form, and ask them to classify all books on the list. In addition, ask them to describe the genres using key details based on their understanding. Provide light support, if needed.

    • Allow students 15 minutes to accomplish the genre task. Circulate through the room as they are labeling descriptions in the form and listen in on their partner talk to see if they are making correct judgments about the genres.

      Some students may need to work with a partner who can help them read the titles so that they can sort them. In addition, students may find it easier to accomplish this task as a matching activity.
