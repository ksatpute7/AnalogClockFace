Day 3: Spelling Search

Tell students to do a spelling search.

  • Ask them to use a text they are currently reading, one they have already read, or one provided by you. The goal is to find words in the text that fit the spelling patterns and add these to the Spelling Notebook.

  • Ask students to search for three words for each of the following spelling patterns.

    • Find words that have two syllables and follow the Vowel-Consonant-Consonant-Vowel (VCCV) pattern. Remind students that if a word has two syllables, it must have two vowel sounds. Then, have them divide these words into syllables.

      Example: suggest sug – gest
    • Find plural words that end in -s, -es, and -ies. Then, have students find the root word of each plural word.

      Example: wishes/wish
    • Find words that end in -ed, -ing, -er, and -est. Then, have students find the root word of each of these words.

      Example: dropped/drop
    • Find words that are digraphs; they have two vowels that combine to form a long vowel sound. “When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking.”

      Examples: boat, seed, clean
    • Find words that are diphthongs: they have two vowels that glide into one sound.

      Example: about, brown
