Introduction to The Rough-Face Girl

Now that you have explored a Cinderella story from one culture, it is time to hear a version from another culture.

As you listen to your teacher read The Rough-Face Girl aloud, think about how this story compares to Cinderella by Eleanor Fremont.

Now discuss the following questions with your class.

  • Why is she called “Rough-Face Girl”?
  • How are the sisters united against the Rough-Face Girl?
  • Why do the sisters think they will marry the invisible being?
  • Do they think they can trick the invisible being’s sister? How?
  • What does the Rough-Face Girl use to make a new outfit? How do the people in her community react to her new clothes? Compare their reaction to other stories you have read in this unit.
  • How is this story similar to the other Cinderella story and how is it different?
  • How is The Rough-Face Girl like Crow Boy?
